# Apply for Local Scholarships — City of Koronadal

Use this guide to find **City/LGU**, **partner**, and **national** scholarship options you can apply for while based in the City of Koronadal. Local scholarship and educational assistance programs are coordinated through the **City Mayor's Office** and the **City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO)**.

---

## 1) City/LGU Scholarship and Educational Assistance Programs

The City Government of Koronadal periodically opens **scholarship slots and educational assistance** for qualified residents, especially students from low-income households. Calls for applications, slots, coverage, and deadlines vary per cycle and are announced through official city channels.

| Program                                | Who it's for                                                      | What it may cover                                               | Where to apply                                   |
| -------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **City scholarship / educational aid** | Bona fide Koronadal residents (priority to low-income households) | Tuition assistance or educational allowance, depending on cycle | City Mayor's Office / CSWDO, per the posted call |

> Coverage and slots vary by year and partner school. Always verify against the current official announcement before applying.

**How to apply (typical flow)**

1. Watch for the **official call for applications** from the City Government of Koronadal (the City Mayor's Office, CSWDO, or City Information Office).
2. Prepare your documents (see **Common Requirements** below).
3. Submit your application at the designated **city window or online form** and attend evaluation or orientation if shortlisted.

---

## 2) National Programs You Can Apply For While Based in Koronadal

These are national scholarship programs open to qualified students anywhere in the Philippines, including Koronadal residents.

| Program                                 | Who it's for                                                           | Typical coverage                                         | Where to file                                                       |
| --------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **CHED Merit Scholarship (CMSP)**       | Incoming/continuing college students meeting merit and income criteria | Stipend plus tuition/book assistance, per CHED rules     | CHED's official calls and portals (watch the national CHED page)    |
| **DOST-SEI Undergraduate Scholarship**  | Grade 12 / incoming college students in priority S&T courses           | Tuition, stipend, and allowances, per DOST-SEI rules     | DOST-SEI official application portal during the annual call         |
| **OWWA EDSP/ODSP** (for OFW dependents) | Qualified dependents of active OWWA members                            | Educational benefit per the program's current guidelines | OWWA Scholarship portal; the local PESO often posts the call window |

> Amounts and timelines follow each agency's current guidelines. Always read the official call.

## Common Requirements (have these ready)

- **Proof of residency** (Barangay Certificate)
- **Birth certificate** (PSA)
- **Report card/grades** (JHS/SHS/college)
- **Good moral certificate** (from school)
- **Income documents** (parents' ITR / Certificate of Non-Filing / Certificate of Indigency, depending on the program)
- **Photos/ID** (2×2 or passport size)
- **Admission/Enrollment proof** (if already admitted)

(Exact lists vary per program; always follow the posted checklist.)

---

## Step-by-Step: How to Apply

1. **Pick your target programs**
   - The city scholarship/educational assistance (watch City Hall channels), your school's in-house scholarship, and at least one national program.

2. **Check the current call**
   - Read the **eligibility, coverage, and deadline**. Save the announcement.

3. **Assemble documents**
   - Use the checklist above; request certificates early (Barangay, School Registrar, and income documents).

4. **Submit**
   - Follow the **specified portal or window**. Keep digital scans (PDF) ready for online filing.

5. **Monitor and attend screening**
   - Answer calls/emails, attend orientation or interviews, and **sign the scholarship agreement** if accepted.

6. **Comply every term**
   - Maintain the **required grades**, submit grade reports on time, and **renew** each semester or year as required.

## Where to Watch for Announcements

- **City Mayor's Office** — city scholarship and educational assistance calls
- **City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO)** — educational assistance for indigent students
- **City Information Office** — official city-wide announcements
- **PESO Koronadal** — national program windows (such as OWWA and SPES)
- **DepEd Koronadal City Division** and your **target school's registrar/scholarship office** — school-based and national S&T scholarships
