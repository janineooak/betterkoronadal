# Rent Stalls in Public Markets — City of Koronadal

This guide explains how to apply for and rent a stall in a public market operated by the City of Koronadal. Public markets and other city economic enterprises are managed by the **City Economic Enterprise Development Office (CEEDO)**, headed by Kirbie Mae Pacinabao-Obando.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Check for available stalls

- Visit or call the **CEEDO** to ask which markets and sections have vacant stalls and the applicable rental rates.
- Note the type of goods allowed for each market section (for example, wet, dry, or general merchandise).

### Step 2: File your application

- Fill out the **stall application form** at the CEEDO.
- Submit your valid ID, business registration (DTI for sole proprietors), and barangay clearance.

### Step 3: Evaluation and award

- The office reviews applications based on available stalls, eligibility, and the city's market rules.
- If approved, you will be notified to sign a **lease/occupancy agreement** for the stall.

### Step 4: Pay fees and occupy the stall

- Pay the required deposit and rental fees at the **City Treasurer's Office**.
- Keep your official receipts and comply with market rules on operating hours, cleanliness, and the goods you may sell.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Accomplished stall application form — from the CEEDO
- [ ] Valid government-issued ID
- [ ] Business name registration (DTI for sole proprietors), where applicable
- [ ] Barangay clearance

### Eligibility and Rules

- Applicants must intend to personally operate the stall and sell goods allowed for that market section.
- Stallholders must keep rentals and fees current and follow market ordinances on sanitation, hours, and conduct.
- Subleasing or transferring a stall without the city's approval is generally not allowed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much is the stall rental?**
A: Rental rates and fees are set by the city's Revenue Code and vary by market and stall type. Contact the CEEDO for the current rates.

**Q: What if no stalls are available?**
A: You may be placed on a waiting list. Ask the CEEDO how vacancies are announced and prioritized.

**Q: Can I sell any product in my stall?**
A: No. Each market section has designated goods (for example, wet or dry goods). Confirm the allowed goods with the CEEDO before applying.
