# Ask for Free Seeds, Seedlings, Fertilizers, or Fingerlings — City of Koronadal

The City Agriculture Office of Koronadal helps farmers and fisherfolk by distributing free or subsidized agricultural inputs when these are available. These may include rice and vegetable seeds, fruit and tree seedlings, fertilizers, and fingerlings for fishponds, supplied through the city and through national programs of the Department of Agriculture (DA) and the Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources (BFAR).

## Who Can Apply

- Farmers and fisherfolk who are residents of Koronadal.
- Registered members of the Registry System for Basic Sectors in Agriculture (RSBSA) or local farmer/fisherfolk lists are usually prioritized.
- Farmers' associations and irrigators' or fisherfolk organizations may apply on behalf of their members for certain programs.

---

## How to Apply

1. **Visit the City Agriculture Office**
   - Ask which inputs are currently available (seeds, seedlings, fertilizers, or fingerlings).
   - Ask whether the distribution is open to individuals, associations, or specific barangays.

2. **Register or update your records**
   - If you are not yet listed, register as a farmer or fisherfolk (for example, under the RSBSA).
   - Make sure your contact details and farm or fishpond information are up to date.

3. **Submit your requirements**
   - Provide proof of identity and residency.
   - Provide proof that you are an active farmer or fisherfolk, such as your registration record.

4. **Claim your inputs**
   - Wait for the distribution schedule announced by the City Agriculture Office.
   - Claim your allocation and follow any guidance the agricultural technicians provide on planting or stocking.

---

## Important Reminders

- **Free and subsidized inputs are limited.** Slots and quantities depend on available stock and program guidelines.
- **Never pay anyone** to be added to a beneficiary list for free government inputs.
- **Register early.** Being listed as a farmer or fisherfolk helps you qualify for future distributions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Are the seeds and fertilizers really free?**
A: Many inputs are free or subsidized through city and national programs, but availability depends on stock and program funds. Confirm with the City Agriculture Office.

**Q: How do I get on the list of beneficiaries?**
A: Register as a farmer or fisherfolk with the City Agriculture Office, for example through the RSBSA, and keep your records updated.

**Q: Can my farmers' association apply for the whole group?**
A: Yes, some programs allow associations to apply for their members. Ask the City Agriculture Office about the current rules.
