# Borrow Farm Tools or Equipment from the LGU — City of Koronadal

The City Agriculture Office of Koronadal helps farmers and farmers' associations access farm machinery and equipment, such as those provided through the Department of Agriculture's farm mechanization programs. Depending on availability, you may be able to borrow, rent at a low cost, or schedule the use of shared equipment to make your farm work easier and more efficient.

## Who Can Borrow

- Farmers and farmers' associations who are residents of Koronadal.
- Registered farmer or irrigators' associations, which are often prioritized for shared machinery.
- Individual farmers, for certain tools, depending on the program rules.

---

## Examples of Available Equipment

Depending on what the city has on hand, you may be able to access:

- **Land preparation equipment** such as hand tractors or four-wheel tractors.
- **Planting and harvesting machines** such as transplanters, harvesters, or threshers.
- **Post-harvest equipment** such as mechanical dryers or shellers.
- **Smaller tools and implements** for farm work.

> The specific equipment available, the terms of use, and any fees depend on program guidelines and the condition and availability of the units. Confirm with the City Agriculture Office.

---

## How to Borrow

1. **Visit or contact the City Agriculture Office**
   - Ask which tools or equipment are available and the terms for borrowing or renting.
   - Ask whether the equipment is for individuals or for associations.

2. **Register or coordinate as a farmer or association**
   - If you are not yet listed, register as a farmer or fisherfolk (for example, under the RSBSA).
   - For machinery, the office may require you to apply through a registered farmers' association.

3. **Submit your requirements and request**
   - Provide proof of identity and residency.
   - Submit a request or reservation form, including the dates and the area you plan to work on.

4. **Schedule and use the equipment**
   - Wait for confirmation of your booking and the schedule.
   - Use the equipment according to the agreed terms, and return it on time and in good condition.

## Important Reminders

- **Return equipment on time and in good condition** so other farmers can use it.
- **Report any damage or problems** to the City Agriculture Office right away.
- **Terms and fees depend on the program.** Some equipment is free to use, while others may have a minimal fee for fuel or maintenance. Confirm before you book.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is borrowing farm equipment free?**
A: Some equipment is free to use, while others may have a minimal fee for fuel or upkeep. Confirm the terms with the City Agriculture Office.

**Q: Can I borrow as an individual, or do I need to be in an association?**
A: Smaller tools may be available to individuals, but larger machinery is often coursed through registered farmers' associations. Ask the City Agriculture Office about the rules for the equipment you need.

**Q: How early should I book?**
A: Book as early as possible, especially during planting and harvest seasons when demand is high.
