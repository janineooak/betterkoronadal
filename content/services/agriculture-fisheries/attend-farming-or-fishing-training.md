# Attend Farming or Fishing Training — City of Koronadal

The City Agriculture Office of Koronadal offers training and technical guidance to help farmers and fisherfolk improve their yields and incomes. Trainings cover modern and sustainable techniques in crop production, livestock raising, and aquaculture, and are often delivered with national partners such as the Department of Agriculture (DA) and the Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources (BFAR).

## Who Can Join

- Farmers and fisherfolk who are residents of Koronadal.
- Members of farmers' associations, irrigators' associations, and fisherfolk organizations.
- Aspiring farmers, agri-entrepreneurs, and family members helping in farm or fishing operations.

---

## Examples of Training Topics

Depending on what is currently offered, trainings may cover:

- **Crop production** such as rice, corn, and high-value vegetables, including good agricultural practices.
- **Livestock and poultry raising** and basic animal health (in coordination with the City Veterinary Office).
- **Aquaculture and fishing** techniques, including fishpond management and sustainable practices (often with BFAR).
- **Farm management and marketing**, including post-harvest handling and access to programs and credit.

> The exact topics, schedules, and slots depend on program guidelines and partner agencies. Confirm what is currently open with the City Agriculture Office.

---

## How to Join

1. **Contact or visit the City Agriculture Office**
   - Ask which trainings are currently scheduled and which topics they cover.
   - Ask about the venue, schedule, and any requirements.

2. **Register for a training**
   - Sign up for the training you are interested in.
   - If you are not yet listed as a farmer or fisherfolk, register (for example, under the RSBSA).

3. **Submit your requirements**
   - Provide proof of identity and residency.
   - Provide your farmer or fisherfolk registration, if required.

4. **Attend the training**
   - Attend the scheduled sessions and any field demonstrations.
   - Apply what you learn, and ask the agricultural technicians for follow-up support.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is the training free?**
A: Government farming and fishing trainings are usually free. Confirm with the City Agriculture Office when you register.

**Q: I am new to farming. Can I still join?**
A: Yes. Many trainings welcome aspiring and beginning farmers. Ask the City Agriculture Office which sessions fit your level.

**Q: How will I know when the next training is?**
A: Contact the City Agriculture Office or follow the City Government of Koronadal Facebook page for announcements.
