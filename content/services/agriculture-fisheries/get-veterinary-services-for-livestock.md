# Get Veterinary Services for Livestock — City of Koronadal

The City Veterinary Office of Koronadal helps livestock and poultry raisers keep their animals healthy. The office provides services such as vaccination, deworming, treatment, and advice on animal care, and it supports disease control efforts in coordination with the Department of Agriculture (DA) and the Bureau of Animal Industry.

## Who Can Avail

- Livestock and poultry raisers who are residents of Koronadal.
- Backyard and small-scale farmers raising cattle, carabao, goats, swine, poultry, and other animals.
- Pet owners, for certain services such as anti-rabies vaccination.

---

## Services Offered

Depending on what is currently available, the City Veterinary Office may provide:

- **Vaccination** for livestock, poultry, and pets (including anti-rabies vaccination for dogs and cats).
- **Deworming and treatment** of sick or injured animals.
- **Animal health advice** on feeding, housing, and disease prevention.
- **Disease surveillance and control**, especially during outbreaks, in coordination with national agencies.
- **Assistance with shipping or movement of animals**, such as veterinary inspection requirements.

> The exact services available, and any fees, depend on program guidelines and supplies on hand. Confirm with the City Veterinary Office.

---

## How to Get Service

1. **Contact or visit the City Veterinary Office**
   - Describe your animals and the service you need (for example, vaccination or treatment).
   - Ask whether the service is done at the office or through a scheduled field visit.

2. **Provide information about your animals**
   - Give the number and type of animals and where they are located.
   - For a sick animal, describe the symptoms so the staff can prepare.

3. **Schedule the service**
   - For field activities such as mass vaccination, the office may set a barangay schedule.
   - Follow the date and any preparation instructions given to you.

4. **Receive the service and follow-up advice**
   - Animals are vaccinated, treated, or examined as needed.
   - Follow the care instructions and any schedule for booster shots or follow-up.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is anti-rabies vaccination for my dog free?**
A: The City Veterinary Office holds anti-rabies vaccination activities, which are often free or low-cost. Confirm the schedule and any fee with the office.

**Q: My animal is sick. Can the vet visit my farm?**
A: The office handles some cases at the office and others through field visits. Contact the City Veterinary Office to describe the situation and arrange the right response.

**Q: I need a veterinary clearance to ship animals. Where do I go?**
A: Visit the City Veterinary Office and ask about the requirements for animal movement or shipping permits.
