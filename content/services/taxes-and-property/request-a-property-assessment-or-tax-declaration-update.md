# Request a Property Assessment or Tax Declaration Update — City of Koronadal

Built a new house, installed machinery, discovered untitled land, or transferred, subdivided, or consolidated a property? The City Assessor's Office handles new assessments and updates to your Tax Declaration — including transfers, segregation/consolidation, re-assessment, and the annotation or cancellation of liens. This guide walks you through each one.

## How to Apply (general flow)

All of these services share the same starting flow at the City Assessor's Office:

### Step 1: Get and fill out the Request Form

- Secure the Request Form and list of requirements and fill it out. (About 5 minutes)

### Step 2: Submit your form and requirements

- Submit the completed form with your documents. Staff check for correctness and completeness (about 10 to 15 minutes) and verify the property in PATAS and archives.
- The Request Form is returned to you as the order of payment, directing you to the City Treasurer's Office.

### Step 3: Pay the fees

- Pay the corresponding fees at the City Treasurer's Office and get your Official Receipt.

### Step 4: Return for processing

- Submit your Official Receipt at the City Assessor's Office. For services requiring a site visit, you receive a claim slip and the inspection is scheduled within 3 days (you will be informed by text or call).

### Step 5: Claim your document

- Once approved and printed, claim your updated Tax Declaration, Notice of Assessment, annotated document, or Notice of Cancellation.

## Assessment of New Buildings, Machineries, and Newly Discovered Land (Charter Item 32)

Owners must declare the true value of newly constructed buildings and machineries, and newly discovered land, so they can be assessed for taxation. A site inspection is conducted (within 3 days of your request) for properties declared for the first time.

**Requirements include:**

- Duly accomplished Request Form
- Proof of Billing (one photocopy)
- Lease Contract (optional); SPA and/or authorization letter if a representative
- **If a Building:** Approved Building Permit, Approved Building Plan, and Bill of Materials (one machine copy each) — from the Office of the Building Official (OBO)
- **If Machinery:** Acquisition cost, purchase receipt, installation and freight cost
- **If Newly Discovered Land:** Survey Plan by a licensed Geodetic Engineer approved by the DENR-LMB; CENRO (Banga) certification that the land is alienable & disposable; affidavit/sworn statement of ownership and possession; Barangay certification; NCIP certification (where applicable); and a free patent, homestead, or miscellaneous sales application

**Fees:** Inspection Fee of PHP 60.00 (0–5 km from City Hall), PHP 100.00 (5–10 km), or PHP 150.00 (more than 10 km).

**Processing time (classified Highly Technical):** about 3 days, 6 hours and 44 minutes for simple structures; 7 days, 6 hours and 44 minutes for complex structures; or 15 days, 6 hours and 44 minutes for highly technical structures.

## Tax Declaration for Transfer of Assessment, Segregation/Consolidation, and Re-Assessment (Charter Item 33)

Anyone who transfers real property ownership must notify the City Assessor within 60 days of the transfer. This service also covers segregation/consolidation of lots, depreciation of buildings and machineries, and re-classification of lots. Properties for re-assessment or re-classification are inspected (within 3 days of your request).

**Requirements include:**

- Duly accomplished Request Form
- Electronic copy of title
- Deed of conveyance (one machine copy) — from the Registry of Deeds
- Certificate Authorizing Registration (one machine copy) — from the BIR
- Latest Tax Clearance — from the City Treasurer's Office
- Proof of Billing; SPA and/or authorization letter if a representative
- **For Reclassification/Depreciation:** Request letter from the owner; Zoning Certification from CPDO
- **For Segregation/Consolidation:** Approved Survey Plan (DENR blueprint copy) and Approved Survey Plan (LRA certified copy) from a Geodetic Engineer
- **For untitled properties:** Transfer Tax receipt (one machine copy) from the City Treasurer's Office

**Fees:**

- Transfer of Tax Declaration — PHP 50.00
- Reassessment at owner's request — PHP 50.00
- Verification Fee — PHP 50.00
- Assessment of Subdivision Plan: less than ten (10) parcels — PHP 100.00; ten (10) parcels and above — PHP 100.00
- Late Filing Standard Fine — PHP 200.00 + ½ of 1% of the total assessment value
- Inspection Fee — PHP 60.00 (0–5 km), PHP 100.00 (5–10 km), or PHP 150.00 (more than 10 km)

**Processing time (classified Highly Technical):** about 3 days, 6 hours and 44 minutes (simple); 7 days, 6 hours and 44 minutes (complex); or 15 days, 6 hours and 44 minutes (highly technical structures).

## Annotation/Cancellation of Liens and Encumbrances (Charter Item 28)

This lets property owners reflect the annotation or cancellation of liens and encumbrances (such as mortgages) on their Tax Declaration, based on a Notice of Cancellation of Mortgage, a Mortgage Agreement, or a Court Order.

**Requirements:**

- Duly accomplished Request Form
- Original or Transfer Certificate of Title (one photocopy) — from the Registry of Deeds
- Mortgage Agreement/Court Order for annotation (one original + one photocopy) — from the bank/Registry of Deeds
- Notice of Cancellation of Mortgage/Court Order for cancellation (one original + one photocopy) — from the bank/Registry of Deeds

**Fees (annotation/cancellation of mortgages/encumbrances, based on the amount of consideration):**

| Consideration                        | Fee        |
| ------------------------------------ | ---------- |
| Annotation/Cancellation of Bail Bond | PHP 50.00  |
| Less than PHP 10,000.00              | PHP 50.00  |
| PHP 10,001.00 to PHP 100,000.00      | PHP 100.00 |
| PHP 100,001.00 to PHP 500,000.00     | PHP 250.00 |
| PHP 500,001.00 to One Million        | PHP 300.00 |
| One Million One to 5 Million         | PHP 400.00 |
| 5 Million One and above              | PHP 500.00 |

**Processing time (classified Simple):** about 48 minutes.

## Cancellation of Assessment (Charter Item 31)

When several assessments are made on one and the same property, the Assessor cancels all but the one properly made. A site inspection is conducted (within 3 days of your request) before the Notice of Cancellation is issued.

**Requirements:**

- Duly accomplished Request Form
- Government-issued valid ID (one photocopy)
- Request letter from the declared owner
- Latest Tax Clearance — from the City Treasurer's Office
- If not the owner: representative's valid ID with signature (one photocopy) and an SPA and/or authorization letter

**Fees:** Inspection Fee — PHP 60.00 (0–5 km from City Hall), PHP 100.00 (5–10 km), or PHP 150.00 (more than 10 km).

**Processing time (classified Complex):** about 8 hours, with the property subjected to inspection within 3 days of your request.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I just built a new house. Do I need to declare it?**
A: Yes. Owners must declare newly constructed buildings (and machineries) so they can be assessed for taxation. Bring your approved Building Permit, Building Plan, and Bill of Materials from the OBO.

**Q: I bought a property. How soon must I notify the Assessor?**
A: Within 60 days of the transfer. Late filing incurs a standard fine of PHP 200.00 plus ½ of 1% of the total assessment value.

**Q: Why do some requests take days instead of minutes?**
A: New assessments and transfers/segregation are "Highly Technical" and require field inspection, FAAS preparation, and approval. Timelines depend on whether your structure is simple (about 3 days), complex (about 7 days), or highly technical (about 15 days).

**Q: How much does it cost to annotate or cancel a mortgage on my Tax Declaration?**
A: The fee ranges from PHP 50.00 to PHP 500.00 depending on the amount of consideration. See the table above.

**Q: Will I be charged for the inspection?**
A: Yes, when a site visit is required: PHP 60.00 within 5 km of City Hall, PHP 100.00 for 5–10 km, and PHP 150.00 beyond 10 km.
