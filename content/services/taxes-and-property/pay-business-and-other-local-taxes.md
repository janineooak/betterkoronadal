# Pay Business and Other Local Taxes — City of Koronadal

The City Treasurer's Office collects business tax and a range of other local taxes, fees, and charges — including transfer tax, situs tax, weights-and-measures sealing fees, idle land tax, the Community Tax Certificate (cedula), and more. This guide explains how to pay each one and what it costs.

## How to Apply (general flow)

Most tax payments at the City Treasurer's Office follow the same simple flow:

### Step 1: Get a queuing number

- Secure a queuing number for billing and payment from the kiosk and wait for it to be called. (About 1 minute)

### Step 2: Present your requirements

- Proceed to the designated window and present your requirements.
- The Billing Clerk checks requirements, verifies records, computes your tax, fees, and charges, and issues your tax bill. (About 5 to 30 minutes depending on the tax)

### Step 3: Pay and secure your Official Receipt

- Pay the corresponding tax, fees, and charges to the Tax Collector and secure your Official Receipt. (About 2 to 5 minutes)
- **Always keep your Official Receipt.** For some taxes (e.g. business tax, situs tax) you must submit the OR to the relevant office afterward (BPLO or City Engineer's Office).

## Business Tax (Charter Item 202)

Business tax is imposed on persons who establish, operate, conduct, or maintain a business within the city. The same flow above applies to **new** businesses, **renewals**, and **quarterly** payments.

**Requirements:**

- Copy of your latest Official Receipt (one photocopy)
- Copy of your Business Permit (one original for verification + one photocopy) — from CMO-BPLD
- Business Account Number (one photocopy)

**Fees:** Business tax is a **graduated tax** based on your gross sales or receipts for the preceding calendar year, and the rate schedule depends on your type of business. Examples from the charter:

- **Retailers:** 2.4% for gross sales of PHP 400,000.00 or less; PHP 9,600.00 + 1.2% of the excess over PHP 400,000.00.
- **Restaurants, hotels, amusement places, and most other businesses:** 2.5% of the preceding year's gross receipts.
- **Lessors of real estate and hospitals/clinics:** 1.65% of the preceding year's gross receipts.
- **Banks and financial institutions:** 60% of 1% of gross receipts.
- **Peddlers:** PHP 150.00 per peddler annually.
- **Newly started business:** initial tax of 1/10 of 1% of the capital investment for the starting quarter.

Manufacturers, wholesalers/distributors, and contractors follow detailed bracketed schedules in the charter. Ask the Billing Clerk to compute your exact amount. **Late payment** carries a 25% surcharge plus 2% interest per month on the unpaid amount (interest capped at 36 months).

## Transfer Tax (Charter Item 205)

Paid by a property owner who wants to transfer ownership of real property.

**Requirements:**

- Latest Tax Declaration (Lot and Improvement) — one photocopy, from the City Assessor's Office
- Latest Certificate of No Improvement (if none) — one photocopy, from the City Assessor's Office
- Transfer documents from the original owner (Deed of Sale, Transfer/Waiver of Rights, Extrajudicial Settlement, and other supporting legal documents) — one photocopy
- Certificate of Title — one photocopy, from the Registry of Deeds

**Fees:**

- **Within 60 days of the notarial date:** 75% of 1% of the total amount of consideration or fair market value, whichever is higher.
- **After 60 days of the notarial date:** 75% x 1% of the consideration or FMV (whichever is higher) + 25% surcharge + 2% penalty per month of the tax due and surcharge.

> Tip: Pay within 60 days of the notarial date to avoid the surcharge and monthly penalty. (Processing total: about 72 minutes.)

## Situs Tax (Charter Item 206)

A business tax collected from contractors and similar businesses maintaining or operating a branch, sales outlet, or project within Koronadal City.

**Requirements:**

- Bill of Materials (one photocopy) — from the City Engineer's Office
- Duly signed project contract with the infrastructure owner (one original for verification + one photocopy) — from the City Engineer's Office

**Fees:**

- **Project cost more than PHP 2,000,000.00:** Tax base is 70% of total project cost. The portion up to PHP 2,000,000.00 is taxed at PHP 13,800.00, plus the excess (remaining project cost) x 60% of 1%.
- **Project cost PHP 2,000,000.00 or less:** Tax base is 70% of total project cost; computed using the business tax graduated schedule for contractors and other independent contractors.

Submit your Official Receipt to the City Engineer's Office after paying.

## Annual Tax for Sealing and Licensing of Weights and Measures (Charter Item 207)

All instruments used to determine weights and measures in consumer transactions must be tested, calibrated, and sealed every six (6) months by the City Treasurer or an authorized representative.

**Requirements:**

- The weighing/measuring unit itself — bring it to the City Treasurer's Office
- Previous sealing sticker (one original)

**Steps:** Get a queuing number, present your unit for inspection and calibration, secure the order of payment, pay the fees, then present your Official Receipt for sealing. (Processing total: about 26 minutes.)

**Selected fees (each plus a Paper Seal fee of PHP 35.00):**

| Instrument                                  | Fee (+ Paper Seal PHP 35.00) |
| ------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Linear measure not over 1 meter             | PHP 100.00                   |
| Linear measure over 1 meter                 | PHP 200.00                   |
| Weighing scale, capacity ≤ 30 kg            | PHP 100.00                   |
| Weighing scale, > 30 kg to ≤ 300 kg         | PHP 300.00                   |
| Weighing scale, > 300 kg to ≤ 3,000 kg      | PHP 600.00                   |
| Weighing scale, > 3,000 kg                  | PHP 1,000.00                 |
| Gasoline/Diesel/Oil pump, per pump          | PHP 200.00                   |
| Oil dispenser pump, per pump                | PHP 100.00                   |
| Electric meter — residential                | Exempted                     |
| Electric meter — commercial                 | PHP 200.00                   |
| Electric meter — industrial / institutional | PHP 500.00                   |

> Expired weights-and-measures units are charged an additional 25% surcharge + 2% interest per month.

## Idle Land and Commercial/Industrial Taxes (Charter Item 208)

An additional annual Ad Valorem tax levied on idle lands and idle commercial/industrial buildings that remain unutilized for more than one year.

**Requirements:**

- Copy of your latest RPT Official Receipt (one photocopy)
- Copy of your Tax Declaration (one original) — from the City Assessor's Office

**Fees:**

- **Idle Land:** Assessed Value x 5%
- **Idle Commercial/Industrial Building:** Assessed Value x 2%

(Processing total: about 11 minutes.)

## Other Taxes, Fees and Charges (Charter Item 209)

This covers the Community Tax Certificate (cedula), delivery truck/van tax, professional tax, occupational tax ID, amusement tax, sidewalk/ambulant vendor fees, accountable forms, and other fees from various offices.

**Requirements vary by item**, for example:

- **Community Tax Certificate (individual):** valid ID. **(Corporation):** preceding year gross sales.
- **Delivery trucks and vans:** latest LTO Official Receipt and Certificate of Registration.
- **Tax on Professional:** PRC ID or any government-issued ID.
- **Occupational Tax ID:** valid ID (tricycle drivers included).
- **Amusement Tax:** sales report; for cockpit/derby, a letter request approved by the LCE.

**Fees:**

| Item                                      | Fee                                                                    |
| ----------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Community Tax Certificate — Individual    | PHP 5.00 basic + PHP 1.00 per PHP 1,000.00 income (max PHP 5,005.00)   |
| Community Tax Certificate — Corporation   | PHP 500.00 + PHP 2.00 per PHP 5,000.00 gross sales (max PHP 10,500.00) |
| Annual Fixed Tax for Delivery Trucks/Vans | PHP 650.00 + PHP 200.00 sticker                                        |
| Tax on Professional                       | PHP 300.00                                                             |
| Occupational Tax ID                       | PHP 200.00                                                             |
| Amusement Tax                             | Gross sales x 2.5%                                                     |
| Sidewalk / Ambulant Vendor Fee            | PHP 10.00 x area (square meter)                                        |
| Accountable Forms                         | PHP 150.00 per stub                                                    |
| Other fees from other offices             | Based on the order of payment issued by the responsible office         |

> Penalties: Community Tax Certificate paid after the March 31 deadline incurs 2% interest per month; delivery truck/van tax paid after January 31 incurs a 25% surcharge + 2% interest per month.

## Fees

Each tax has its own fees, listed in the sections above. Where the charter does not state a specific amount, refer to the City Revenue Code or ask the office.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How is business tax computed?**
A: It is a graduated tax based on your gross sales or receipts from the preceding calendar year, with rates depending on your type of business (for example, 2.5% for restaurants and most other businesses, or 2.4% for small retailers). The Billing Clerk computes your exact amount.

**Q: When is the deadline for the Community Tax Certificate (cedula)?**
A: March 31. Payments made after that incur 2% interest per month.

**Q: I run a sari-sari store — do I pay business tax to the city?**
A: Barangays have the exclusive power to tax stores whose preceding-year gross sales do not exceed PHP 50,000.00. Larger businesses are taxed by the city.

**Q: How often do I need my weighing scale sealed?**
A: Every six (6) months. Bring the unit and your previous sealing sticker to the City Treasurer's Office.

**Q: How do I avoid penalties on my transfer tax?**
A: Pay within 60 days of the notarial date. After 60 days you incur a 25% surcharge plus a 2% monthly penalty.
