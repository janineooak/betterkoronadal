# Get a Tax Declaration or Property Certificate — City of Koronadal

Need a certified copy of your Tax Declaration, or a certification such as Total Landholding, No Landholding, or No Improvement? The City Assessor's Office issues these documents for declared property owners and their authorized representatives. This guide covers how to request them and what they cost.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Get and fill out the Request Form

- Secure the Request Form and the list of requirements from the City Assessor's Office and fill it out. (About 5 minutes)

### Step 2: Submit your form and requirements

- Submit the completed Request Form together with your requirements.
- Records Personnel check the form for correctness and completeness (about 10 minutes) and verify the document's availability in the Property Assessment and Tax Administration System (PATAS). (About 15 minutes)
- They return the Request Form, which serves as your order of payment, and direct you to the City Treasurer's Office to pay. (About 3 minutes)

### Step 3: Pay at the City Treasurer's Office

- Pay the corresponding fees and get your Official Receipt. (See the Fees section.)

### Step 4: Return and submit your Official Receipt

- Return to the City Assessor's Office and submit your Official Receipt.
- Staff prepare and print the requested document (about 3 minutes), and the City Assessor signs it. (About 6 minutes)

### Step 5: Claim your document

- Receive your requested Tax Declaration or certification. (About 5 minutes)

> **For a Certificate of No Improvement:** if the system (GIS) shows an improvement image on the property, the office schedules a site inspection (conducted within 3 days of your request) before issuing the certificate. You will be informed of the schedule by text message or call.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Duly accomplished Request Form (from the City Assessor's Office)
- [ ] Government-issued valid ID (one photocopy)

**If you are not the owner, also bring:**

- [ ] The representative's government-issued valid ID with signature (one photocopy)
- [ ] Special Power of Attorney (SPA) and/or authorization letter (one copy)
- [ ] Deed of conveyance (Deed of Sale, Deed of Donation, Extrajudicial Settlement, Waiver of Rights, etc.) (one photocopy)
- [ ] Other supporting documents may be required

### Eligibility

- Declared property owners or their authorized representatives.

## Fees

**Tax Declarations and certifications (Charter Item 29):**

- Tax Declaration — PHP 100.00
- Additional PHP 30.00 for Documentary Stamp Tax (DST) per certification or tax declaration

**Certificate of No Improvement (Charter Item 30):**

- No Improvement certificate — PHP 100.00
- Additional PHP 30.00 for DST per certification or tax declaration
- Inspection Fee (because a site inspection is required):
  - 0 to 5 km from the City Hall — PHP 60.00
  - 5 km to 10 km — PHP 100.00
  - More than 10 km — PHP 150.00

All fees are paid at the City Treasurer's Office.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What kinds of certifications can I get here?**
A: Certified true copies of your Tax Declaration, and certifications such as Total Landholding, No Landholding, No Improvement, and other property-related certifications listed in the City Assessor's Office.

**Q: Can someone else request my documents for me?**
A: Yes. Your representative must bring their own valid ID with signature, a Special Power of Attorney and/or authorization letter, and a deed of conveyance if applicable.

**Q: Why does a Certificate of No Improvement take longer?**
A: It is classified as a "Complex" transaction. If the system shows a possible improvement on the property, the office must conduct a site inspection — scheduled within 3 days of your request — before issuing the certificate.

**Q: How much is a Tax Declaration copy?**
A: PHP 100.00 plus PHP 30.00 Documentary Stamp Tax per copy.
