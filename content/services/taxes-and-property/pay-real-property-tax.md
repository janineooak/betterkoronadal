# Pay Your Real Property Tax — City of Koronadal

If you own land, a building, or machinery in Koronadal City, you pay your Real Property Tax (RPT) at the City Treasurer's Office. This guide also covers how to get a Real Property Tax Clearance — the certificate proving your property taxes are fully paid and up to date.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Get a queuing number

- Secure a queuing number for billing and payment from the kiosk.
- Wait for your number to be called. (About 1 minute)

### Step 2: Present your requirements at the window

- When your number is called, proceed to the designated window and present your requirements.
- The Billing Clerk checks your requirements, verifies records, computes your tax, fees, and charges, and issues your tax bill. (About 5 minutes)

### Step 3: Pay and get your Official Receipt

- Pay the corresponding Real Property Tax to the Tax Collector.
- The Tax Collector receives your payment and issues an Official Receipt. (About 5 minutes)
- **Always secure your Official Receipt after paying.**

> The same three steps apply whether you are paying on time (prompt payment), paying in advance, or paying late — only the amount computed differs.

## Securing a Real Property Tax Clearance (Charter Item 204)

A Real Property Tax Clearance is issued to property owners whose real property taxes are fully paid and updated. People commonly need it for transfers, loans, leases, zoning, building permits, and other purposes.

**Common requirements (depending on your purpose):**

- Latest Tax Declaration (Lot and Improvement) — one photocopy, from the City Assessor's Office
- Latest Certificate of No Improvement, if there is no improvement — one photocopy, from the City Assessor's Office
- For transfers: Transfer documents (Deed of Sale, Transfer/Waiver of Rights, Extrajudicial Settlement, and other supporting legal documents) and the Certificate of Title from the Registry of Deeds — one photocopy each
- Special Power of Attorney (for representatives), a valid ID, and a Community Tax Certificate, presented upon claiming

**Steps:** Submit your requirements and wait for the order of payment from the Tax Clearance Clerk, pay the corresponding Tax Clearance Fee, then return for preparation, countersigning, and approval. Claim your clearance on the scheduled date by presenting your claim slip and ID.

**Processing time:** A simple certification-type clearance can be prepared within minutes, while a clearance for a Transfer, Segregation, Consolidation, or File can take up to 3 days (the charter lists a total of about 3 days and 31 minutes covering all clearance types).

## Fees

Your Real Property Tax is **computed from the Assessed Value (AV) of your property**, not a flat amount. Based on the Citizen's Charter, the computation works as follows:

- **Prompt (on-time) payment:** Basic Tax Due = AV x 1.1% **less 10%** of the Total Tax Due; SEF (Special Education Fund) Tax Due = AV x 1% **less 10%** of the Total Tax Due.
- **Advance payment:** Basic Tax Due = AV x 1.1% **less 20%** of the Total Tax Due; SEF Tax Due = AV x 1% **less 20%** of the Total Tax Due.
- **Failure to pay on time:** Basic Tax Due = AV x 1.1% **plus 2% interest per month, not exceeding 72%**; SEF Tax Due = AV x 1% **plus 2% interest per month, not exceeding 72%**.

In short: pay on time for a 10% discount, pay in advance for a 20% discount, or face a 2% monthly interest penalty if you pay late. The exact peso amount depends on your property's assessed value — the Billing Clerk computes it for you.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I get a discount if I pay my property tax early?**
A: Yes. The charter provides a 10% discount on the total tax due for prompt (on-time) payment, and a 20% discount for advance payment.

**Q: What happens if I miss the deadline?**
A: Late payments are charged 2% interest per month on the tax due, up to a maximum of 72%.

**Q: Where do I get my Tax Declaration?**
A: From the City Assessor's Office. You need an original copy to pay your Real Property Tax.

**Q: What is the SEF in my tax bill?**
A: The SEF is the Special Education Fund tax, computed at AV x 1%, collected together with your basic real property tax.

**Q: Do I need a Tax Clearance to sell or mortgage my property?**
A: A Real Property Tax Clearance proves your taxes are paid and updated, and is commonly required for transfers, loans, leases, zoning, and building permits. See the section above for requirements and fees.
