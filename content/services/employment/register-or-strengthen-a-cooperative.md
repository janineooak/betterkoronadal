# Register or Strengthen a Cooperative — City of Koronadal

The City Cooperatives Office helps groups form new cooperatives, build the skills of existing ones, and access financial support. Whether you are organizing a brand-new co-op, applying for a capital loan, attending mandatory trainings, or securing an endorsement letter, this office is your starting point.

## How to Apply

### Register a new cooperative (Technical Assistance for COOP Registration)

- **Step 1:** Submit a letter request to the City Cooperatives Office. You will be scheduled for a Cooperative Pre-Registration Seminar.
- **Step 2:** Attend the Pre-Registration Seminar/Webinar (8 hours in person, or 4 hours by webinar).
- **Step 3:** Attend the organizational meeting; the office assists in preparing your registration documents.
- **Step 4:** Submit your documents for final review. The office checks them, then endorses your reviewed registration documents and advises you to submit to the CDA using E-COOPRIS.
- **Step 5:** Once the CDA approves, provide the office with copies of your Certificate of Registration, Articles of Cooperation, and By-laws for filing.

### Apply for a capital loan (Cooperative Loan Assistance Program – CLAP)

- **Step 1:** Submit the required documents for evaluation and wait for a text/call with your inspection schedule. The office conducts an on-site inspection and prepares a Pre-Evaluation Report for the Screening Committee.
- **Step 2:** Attend the Screening Committee meeting. If endorsed, the request goes to the City Mayor and the Sangguniang Panlungsod for Authority to Sign a Memorandum of Agreement (MOA).
- **Step 3:** Attend the committee hearing on the MOA authority if needed, then return on the scheduled MOA signing date.
- **Step 4:** Sign and notarize the MOA, then submit the notarized copies to the City Cooperatives Office.
- **Step 5:** Loan proceeds are processed through the City Budget, Accounting, and Treasurer's Offices. Claim your check at the City Treasurer's Office, sign a promissory note, and issue post-dated checks and an updated Official Receipt.

### Attend cooperative trainings/seminars

- **Step 1:** Submit a letter request duly approved by the City Mayor and wait for your schedule.
- **Step 2:** The office sets the schedule and prepares speakers, materials, and logistics.
- **Step 3:** Attend the training/seminar, then secure your training certificate.

### Secure a Cooperative Endorsement Letter / Certification

- **Step 1:** Submit your documentary requirements (copy of CAPR and a letter request) to the City Cooperatives Office. Staff check them and endorse to the CCDC for approval.
- **Step 2:** Pay the fees at the City Treasurer's Office and keep the Official Receipt.
- **Step 3:** Present your Official Receipt and claim the endorsement letter / certification at the City Cooperatives Office.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed — Technical Assistance for COOP Registration

- [ ] Letter request (1 original)
- [ ] Certificate of Completion on the Pre-Registration Seminar/Webinar (1 original, from CDA)
- [ ] Economic Survey (6 original copies)
- [ ] Articles of Cooperation, all pages signed by all cooperators (6 original copies)
- [ ] Approved By-laws signed by at least a majority of members on the adoption page (6 original copies)
- [ ] Treasurer's Affidavit (6 original copies)
- [ ] Surety Bonds of accountable officers — Chairperson, Treasurer, and Manager (1 original, 5 photocopies)

### Documents Needed — Cooperative Loan Assistance Program (CLAP)

- [ ] Letter of intent to avail of CLAP, addressed to the City Mayor (1 original)
- [ ] Accomplished Loan Application Form (3 original copies, from the City Cooperatives Office)
- [ ] Certified true copy of Certificate of Registration with the CDA (3 photocopies)
- [ ] List of the current Board of Directors and other officers (3 original copies)
- [ ] Latest audited Financial Statement submitted to the CDA, stamped received by the BIR (3 certified photocopies)
- [ ] Project study showing the project profile, capital needed, and income it will generate (3 original copies)
- [ ] Certificate of Compliance issued by the CDA (3 photocopies)
- [ ] Board of Directors Resolution expressing intent to apply for the loan (3 original copies)
- [ ] Accreditation of the Sangguniang Panlungsod (3 photocopies)
- [ ] Once approved: post-dated checks payable to the City Government for quarterly amortization plus interest, and the cooperative's updated Official Receipt for loan proceeds

### Documents Needed — Cooperative Trainings/Seminars

- [ ] Letter request duly approved by the City Mayor (1 original)

### Documents Needed — Cooperative Endorsement Letter / Certification

- [ ] Copy of CAPR (1 original)
- [ ] Letter request (1 original)

### Eligibility

- **Registration assistance:** Proposed cooperatives.
- **Trainings/seminars:** Proposed, micro, and registered cooperatives.
- **CLAP:** All cooperatives — must be duly registered with the CDA, accredited by the City Government of Koronadal, with a main office operating in the City of Koronadal for at least two (2) years.
- **Endorsement letter / certification:** All registered cooperatives.

## Fees

- **Technical Assistance for COOP Registration:** None — this service is free.
- **Cooperative Trainings/Seminars:** None — this service is free.
- **Cooperative Loan Assistance Program (CLAP):** No service fee is listed in the charter; related steps are processed through the City Mayor, Sangguniang Panlungsod, and the City Treasurer's Office.
- **Cooperative Endorsement Letter / Certification:**
  - Endorsement — PHP 200
  - Certification — PHP 100
  - **Total: PHP 300**, paid at the City Treasurer's Office.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: We're just starting a co-op. Where do we begin?**
A: Submit a letter request to the City Cooperatives Office. You will be scheduled for a Cooperative Pre-Registration Seminar, after which the office helps you prepare your registration documents for submission to the CDA via E-COOPRIS.

**Q: How long must our cooperative operate before we can apply for a CLAP loan?**
A: Your co-op must be CDA-registered, accredited by the City Government, and operating in Koronadal with a main office for at least two (2) years.

**Q: Are the trainings free?**
A: Yes. Cooperative trainings/seminars and registration assistance are free.

**Q: How much does an endorsement letter cost?**
A: The endorsement is PHP 200 and a certification is PHP 100 (PHP 300 total if both are needed), paid at the City Treasurer's Office.
