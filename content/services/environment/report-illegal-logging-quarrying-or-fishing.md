# Report Illegal Logging, Quarrying, or Fishing — City of Koronadal

Report illegal logging, quarrying, illegal fishing, and other environmental violations to help protect Koronadal's forests, rivers, and natural resources. The **City Environment and Natural Resources Office (CENRO)**, together with your barangay and partner agencies, acts on reports of activities that damage the environment.

## What You Can Report

- **Illegal logging** — cutting, gathering, or transporting forest products without the proper permit.
- **Illegal quarrying** — extracting sand, gravel, stone, or other materials without authorization.
- **Illegal fishing** — using dynamite, cyanide, fine-mesh nets, or other banned methods, or fishing in protected areas.
- **Other violations** — illegal dumping, burning of waste, encroachment on protected areas, and similar harmful activities.

---

## How to Report

### Step 1: Stay safe first

- Do not confront the people involved. Observe from a safe distance.
- If the activity is ongoing and poses immediate danger, call **Korona-911**.

### Step 2: Gather the key details

Note as much as you safely can:

- **What** is happening (type of violation).
- **Where** it is happening (barangay, purok, and a nearby landmark).
- **When** you saw it (date and time, and whether it is still ongoing).
- **Who** is involved (number of people, vehicles, equipment, plate numbers if visible).
- **Evidence**, such as photos or videos, only if you can take them safely.

### Step 3: Submit your report

- Report to **CENRO** through the City Hall trunkline.
- You may also report to your **barangay council**, which can coordinate with CENRO and partner agencies.
- For urgent or ongoing incidents, call **Korona-911**.

### Step 4: Provide contact details if you can

- Sharing your contact information helps responders verify and follow up.
- If you prefer, you may ask that your identity be kept confidential.

### Step 5: Follow up

- Keep a record of your report details and any reference given to you.
- You may follow up with CENRO through the City Hall trunkline.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I report anonymously?**
A: You can ask that your identity be kept confidential. Sharing your contact details, however, helps responders verify the report and follow up if they need more information.

**Q: What should I do if the activity is happening right now?**
A: Do not confront anyone. From a safe distance, note the details and call **Korona-911** for urgent or ongoing incidents, then report to CENRO or your barangay.

**Q: Do I need evidence to file a report?**
A: Evidence such as photos and the exact location helps, but you should never put yourself at risk to gather it. Report what you safely observed.

**Q: Who acts on these reports?**
A: CENRO coordinates with your barangay and partner agencies to verify and act on environmental violations within the city's authority.
