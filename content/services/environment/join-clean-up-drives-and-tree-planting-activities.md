# Join Clean-Up Drives and Tree Planting Activities — City of Koronadal

Help keep Koronadal clean, green, and resilient by joining community clean-up drives and tree planting activities organized with the **City Environment and Natural Resources Office (CENRO)** and your barangay. These activities protect our waterways, reduce flooding, improve air quality, and bring neighbors together.

---

| Information      | Details                                                                |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |

## Why It Matters

- **Cleaner surroundings** reduce health risks and prevent clogged drainage that causes flooding.
- **Trees** provide shade, clean the air, cool the city, and help hold soil in place.
- **Proper waste handling** during clean-ups keeps recyclables out of landfills and waterways.
- **Community participation** builds shared responsibility for the environment.

---

## How to Join

### Step 1: Find an activity

- Ask your **barangay hall** about upcoming clean-up drives and tree planting schedules.
- Follow the **City Government of Koronadal** Facebook page for announcements.
- Contact **CENRO** through the City Hall trunkline if your school, workplace, or group wants to organize or join an activity.

### Step 2: Register and coordinate

- Sign up with the organizing barangay or with CENRO so they can plan for the number of volunteers, tools, and seedlings.
- Confirm the meeting place, time, and what you need to bring.

### Step 3: Show up prepared

- Wear comfortable clothes, closed shoes, a hat, and bring water and sun protection.
- Bring your own gloves and reusable water bottle if you can. See the checklist below.

### Step 4: Take part safely and responsibly

- Follow the instructions of the activity leaders.
- During clean-ups, **separate waste** into biodegradable, recyclable, and residual as you collect.
- During tree planting, place each seedling at the right depth and water it as advised.

### Step 5: Help with after-care

- Trees need care to survive. Volunteer to help **water, weed, and protect** young seedlings in the weeks after planting.
- Encourage your household and neighbors to keep the cleaned area clean.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to be part of a group to join?**
A: No. Individuals, families, and volunteers are welcome. Coordinate with your barangay or CENRO so they can prepare enough tools and seedlings.

**Q: Can my school or company organize our own activity?**
A: Yes. Contact CENRO through the City Hall trunkline to coordinate a clean-up or tree planting and to align with the city's environmental programs.

**Q: What happens to the waste we collect?**
A: Collected waste should be segregated into biodegradable, recyclable, and residual. Follow the disposal instructions given by the organizers so recyclables and compostables are handled properly.

**Q: Will the trees we plant be cared for afterward?**
A: Survival depends on after-care. Volunteers and the host barangay are encouraged to help water and protect the seedlings in the months after planting.
