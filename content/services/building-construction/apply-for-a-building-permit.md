# Apply for a Building Permit — City of Koronadal

A Building Permit is the document the **Office of the Building Official (OBO)** issues before you can build, renovate, add to, extend, repair, or alter a structure. The OBO checks that your plans conform to the National Building Code of the Philippines (PD 1096) and its rules before construction starts.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Prepare and submit your documents at the OBO

- Bring your complete requirements to the Office of the Building Official.
- Staff receive and check your documents for completeness and authenticity (about 10 minutes).

### Step 2: Endorsements and validation

- The OBO endorses your Lot Title to the **City Assessor's Office** to validate the latest tax declaration (PhP 100).
- It then endorses to the **City Treasurer's Office** to validate Real Property Tax (RPT) payments (PhP 150 + any RPT due).
- For complex and highly technical applications, the application is also endorsed to the **CPDO–Zoning Division** to check conformance with the Comprehensive Land Use Plan (simple applications skip this endorsement).

### Step 3: Site inspection and technical evaluation

- An inspector conducts a site inspection and prepares an inspection report.
- Engineers evaluate the documents for Line & Grade, Architectural, Structural, Electrical, Mechanical, Sanitary, Plumbing, and Electronics requirements.

### Step 4: Get your Order of Payment

- If approved, the OBO computes your fees and prepares an Order of Payment.
- If there are deficiencies, you will receive a list and your documents back so you can correct and resubmit. (Note: a returned application cuts off the processing clock; resubmission starts a new period.)

### Step 5: Pay at the City Treasurer's Office

- Pay the assessed fees per the New Schedule of Fees, then keep your Official Receipt.

### Step 6: Claim your Building Permit

- Present your Official Receipt at the OBO. The office prepares and issues your Building Permit and supporting documents.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Proof of Ownership over the lot — Certified True Copy of Lot Title from the Registry of Deeds (1 original, 2 photocopies)
- [ ] If you are not the lot owner: Deed of Absolute Sale, Contract of Lease, Extra-Judicial Settlement with Deed of Assignment, Authority-to-Construct, or other notarized document showing your connection to the owner
- [ ] For corporations: Secretary's Certificate or Board Resolution naming the authorized signatory
- [ ] For untitled property: approved subdivision plan, Tax Declaration, and Barangay Certification of actual occupant
- [ ] For representatives: notarized Special Power of Attorney or Authorization Letter with valid IDs
- [ ] Lot Sketch Plan with Vicinity Map and Relocation Survey Plan (or certification of relocation survey) signed and sealed by a Licensed Geodetic Engineer
- [ ] Duly filled-up and notarized Unified Application Form (Building Permit, Locational Clearance, and Fire Safety Evaluation Clearance) plus Ancillary Permit Forms, signed and sealed by the involved professionals and owner (4 original copies)
- [ ] Building Plans / Design Plans (at least A3 size) signed and sealed by the design professional and owner (4 sets)
- [ ] For 2-storey buildings and up: Structural Analysis by a Structural/Civil Engineer (2 sets)
- [ ] For 3-storey buildings and up: Soil Bearing Test (2 sets)
- [ ] For gyms, warehouses, and similar: Structural Analysis (2 sets)
- [ ] Technical Specifications signed and sealed (4 copies)
- [ ] Bill of Materials / Total Construction Cost signed and sealed (4 copies)
- [ ] Valid PRC ID and current Professional Tax Receipt (PTR) with specimen signatures and seals of all involved professionals
- [ ] Fire Safety Evaluation Clearance (FSEC) from the Bureau of Fire Protection (apply via www.fsis.e-bfp.com)
- [ ] Construction Safety and Health Program (CSHP) from DOLE XII (apply via www.reports.dole.gov.ph)
- [ ] Affidavit of Undertaking for other clearances to be submitted within 30 days after permit issuance
- [ ] Expanded envelopes (2 pieces) — green for residential, yellow for commercial, blue for institutional
- [ ] For renovation/addition/extension/repair/alteration: copies of the issued Building Permit plans/forms and the issued FSEC
- [ ] For gas stations, swimming pools, poultry, and piggery: ECC or Certificate of Non-Coverage from DENR-EMB 12 (and an affidavit for private pools)
- [ ] For telecommunication towers/cellsites: Barangay Clearance, CAAP Height Clearance Permit, and ECC if applicable

### Eligibility

- Open to all lot owners and their duly authorized representatives undertaking covered construction work.

## Fees

There is no single fixed price for a Building Permit. Fees are computed under the National Building Code's **New Schedule of Fees and Other Charges**, based on:

- Character of occupancy or use of the building/structure
- Cost of construction
- Floor area
- Height

For example, the schedule sets a fixed cost of construction per square meter (e.g. P10,000 for most occupancy groups, P8,000 for Group F, P6,000 for Group J) and a per-square-meter building fee that varies by division and floor-area bracket. Separate accessory, electrical, line-and-grade, and other fees may also apply.

Small endorsement fees are paid during processing: **PhP 100.00** to the City Assessor's Office (tax-declaration validation) and **PhP 150.00 + any Real Property Tax due** to the City Treasurer's Office.

Because the total depends on your specific project, ask the OBO to compute your exact Order of Payment. Never rely on an estimate alone.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long will it take to get my Building Permit?**
A: It depends on how your project is classified. Simple applications take 3 working days, complex applications 7 working days, and highly technical applications up to 20 working days.

**Q: What makes an application "simple," "complex," or "highly technical"?**
A: Simple applications cover small Group J structures (like a private garage up to 90 sq.m., carports, sheds, agricultural buildings, fences over 1.80 m, tanks and towers) and minor repairs. Complex applications include interior renovations, occupancy conversions, townhouses, and buildings up to 9 storeys. Highly technical applications include buildings over 9 storeys, hospitals, public assembly structures, and special structures. Anything not clearly complex or highly technical is treated as simple.

**Q: What if my application has deficiencies?**
A: It is returned to you with a list of what to fix, and the processing clock is cut off. Once you resubmit with corrections, a new processing period starts.

**Q: Do I need the Fire Safety Evaluation Clearance before applying?**
A: Yes. The FSEC from the Bureau of Fire Protection is part of the checklist; apply for it through www.fsis.e-bfp.com.
