# Apply for Fencing, Electrical, or Demolition Permits — City of Koronadal

The **Office of the Building Official (OBO)** issues several ancillary permits under the National Building Code of the Philippines (PD 1096): a **Fencing Permit** to build a fence, an **Electrical Permit** for traditional indigenous family dwellings, and a **Demolition Permit** to tear down a structure. This guide covers all three.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Submit your documents at the OBO

- Bring the complete requirements for your specific permit (Fencing, Electrical, or Demolition).
- Staff receive and check your documents for completeness and authenticity (about 10 minutes).

### Step 2: Endorsements and validation (Fencing and Demolition)

- For Fencing and Demolition permits, the OBO endorses your Lot Title to the **City Assessor's Office** to validate the latest tax declaration (PhP 100) and to the **City Treasurer's Office** to validate Real Property Tax payments (PhP 120 for Fencing, PhP 150 for Demolition, plus any RPT due).

### Step 3: Site inspection and technical evaluation

- An inspector conducts a site inspection and prepares an inspection report.
- Engineers evaluate the documents (line and grade, structural, electrical, and the applicable technical requirements).

### Step 4: Get your Order of Payment

- If approved, the OBO computes your fees and prepares an Order of Payment.
- If there are deficiencies, you receive a list and your documents back so you can correct and resubmit.

### Step 5: Pay at the City Treasurer's Office

- Pay the assessed fees, then keep your Official Receipt.

### Step 6: Claim your permit

- Present your Official Receipt at the OBO. The office prepares and issues your Fencing, Electrical, or Demolition Permit.

## Requirements

### For a Fencing Permit

- [ ] Duly filled-up and notarized Unified Application Form (Building Permit, Locational Clearance, Fire Safety Evaluation Clearance) and Fencing Permit Forms, signed and sealed (4 copies)
- [ ] Proof of Ownership — Certified True Copy of Lot Title from the Registry of Deeds (1 original, 3 photocopies); plus owner-relationship, corporate, untitled-property, or representative documents as applicable
- [ ] Lot Survey Plan including Sketch Plan and Relocation Survey Plan and Report, signed and sealed by a Licensed Geodetic Engineer
- [ ] Fencing Plans / Design Plans (at least A3 size), signed and sealed (4 sets)
- [ ] Technical Specifications, signed and sealed (4 copies)
- [ ] Bill of Materials / Total Construction Cost, signed and sealed (4 copies)
- [ ] Valid PRC ID and current PTR with specimen signatures and seals of all involved professionals
- [ ] Expanded envelopes (2 pieces) — green for residential, yellow for commercial, blue for institutional enclosures

### For an Electrical Permit (traditional indigenous family dwellings only)

- [ ] Duly filled-up Accomplishment Report (1 set) from the Institute of Integrated Electrical Engineers (IIEE) Office
- [ ] Fire Safety Clearance from the local Bureau of Fire Protection (1 original, 1 photocopy)
- [ ] Certified True Copy of Lot Title from the Registry of Deeds (1 photocopy); plus deed/authority-to-use, lease, or corporate documents if applicable
- [ ] Occupational Tax ID and Community Tax Certificate (cedula) of the electrician who signed the forms
- [ ] Community Tax Certificate (cedula) for the current year (1 photocopy)
- [ ] Photograph of the completed structure showing front, sides, and rear (2 copies)

### For a Demolition Permit

- [ ] Proof of Ownership — Certified True Copy of Lot Title (1 original, 1 photocopy); latest Tax Declaration and Tax Clearance/Receipt of both the lot and the building to be demolished; notarized Authorization Letter for representatives
- [ ] Lot Sketch Plan with Vicinity Map, signed and sealed by a Licensed Geodetic Engineer
- [ ] Duly filled-up and notarized Demolition Permit Form, signed and sealed (4 originals)
- [ ] Building Plans (at least A3 size) showing the floor area and height of the building to be demolished, signed and sealed (4 sets)
- [ ] Colored photos of the building showing front, left, right, and rear (2 copies)
- [ ] Valid PRC ID and current PTR with specimen signatures and seals of all involved professionals
- [ ] Expanded envelopes (2 pieces) — green for residential, yellow for commercial, blue for institutional

### Eligibility

- Open to all lot and building owners and their duly authorized representatives. The Electrical Permit here applies specifically to traditional indigenous family dwellings as described in Section 209 of the National Building Code.

## Fees

**Fencing Permit** — The charter's New Schedule of Fees lists:

- Application Fee: PhP 300.00 (residential) or PhP 500.00 (commercial, institutional, and other uses)
- Inspection Fee: PhP 200.00
- Documentary Stamp: PhP 30.00
- Fencing fees per lineal meter: PhP 3.00 (masonry, metal, concrete up to 1.80 m), PhP 4.00 (in excess of 1.80 m in height), or PhP 2.40 (indigenous materials, barbed/chicken/hog wires)
- Accessory fees for line and grade, pavements, scaffolding, and sidewalk occupancy may also apply
- Endorsement fees during processing: PhP 100.00 (City Assessor) and PhP 120.00 + any RPT due (City Treasurer)

**Electrical Permit** — Computed under the National Building Code electrical fee schedule, based on Total Connected Load (kVA), transformer/UPS/generator capacity, pole/attachment permits, and miscellaneous wiring/meter fees. For example, a connected load of 5 kVA or less is PhP 200.00. The total is the sum of the applicable electrical fee items; ask the OBO for your exact computation.

**Demolition Permit** — Computed under the New Schedule of Fees and Other Charges. Endorsement fees during processing are PhP 100.00 (City Assessor) and PhP 150.00 + any RPT due (City Treasurer). Ask the OBO for your Order of Payment.

Always confirm your exact fees with the office before paying.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need a separate permit just to build a fence?**
A: Yes. A Fencing Permit is issued by the OBO before you construct a fence, with its own plans, application form, and fees.

**Q: Who is the Electrical Permit (this item) for?**
A: This specific Electrical Permit is for owners occupying a traditional indigenous family dwelling, as described in Section 209 of the National Building Code. Electrical work for other buildings is generally handled as part of the Building Permit.

**Q: How long does each permit take?**
A: A Fencing Permit takes 3 working days; the Electrical Permit takes 1 day and 3.5 hours. The charter does not state a total for the Demolition Permit, so ask the OBO directly.

**Q: What if my application has deficiencies?**
A: It is returned with a list of corrections. Once you resubmit with the fixes, a new processing period begins.
