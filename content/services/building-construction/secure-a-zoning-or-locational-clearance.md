# Secure a Zoning or Locational Clearance — City of Koronadal

The **City Planning & Development Office – Zoning Division (CPDO)** confirms that your land or project conforms to the city's Zoning Ordinance (Ordinance No. 6, Series of 2015) and Executive Order No. 72, s. 1993. This guide covers the **Zoning Certification**, the **Locational Clearance for Building and Other Structures**, the **Locational Clearance for Business**, and the **Locational Clearance on Application for Variance/Exception**.

## How to Apply

### Zoning Certification

1. **Fill up and notarize the application form** — Get the form from CPDO–Zoning and have it notarized by a Notary Public, then submit it with your requirements.
2. **Technical evaluation** — A Zoning Officer evaluates your request based on the Zoning Ordinance and may conduct an ocular inspection.
3. **Get your Order of Payment** — The office encodes the application and issues an Order of Payment.
4. **Pay at the City Treasurer's Office** — Pay the Certification Fee (PhP 720 per hectare or fraction) plus the PhP 30 documentary stamp, and secure your Official Receipt.
5. **Claim your Zoning Certificate** — Return to CPDO–Zoning with your receipt; the office prepares, approves, and releases the certificate.

### Locational Clearance for Building and Other Structures

This is part of the building-permit backroom process, so the Office of the Building Official acts as the client.

1. **Submit the first set of requirements (items 1–4)** to CPDO–Zoning for completeness checking.
2. **Technical evaluation** against the Zoning Ordinance; if conforming, the office informs the OBO and issues an additional list of requirements.
3. **Submit the additional requirements (items 5–12)**; the office conducts an ocular inspection and prepares an inspection report.
4. **Get your Order of Payment**, then pay at the City Treasurer's Office.
5. **Return to CPDO–Zoning** with your receipt; the office issues the Locational Clearance and endorses it to the OBO.

### Locational Clearance for Business

1. **Submit your business application** to CPDO–Zoning.
2. **Technical evaluation** against the Zoning Ordinance, with an ocular inspection if necessary.
3. **Claim the Locational Clearance for Business** — if conforming, the office encodes the application and issues the clearance for you to claim and sign for.

### Locational Clearance on Application for Variance/Exception (Highly Technical)

1. **Submit your letter of reconsideration with requirements** — the office evaluates completeness and deliberates the application in a meeting.
2. **Deliberation and inspection** — you may be invited to participate; the Local Zoning Board of Adjustment and Appeals (LZBAA) reviews the application and inspects the project (this stage takes about 15 days).
3. **Get your Order of Payment** if approved (or a denial with the LZBAA resolution if not).
4. **Pay the fees**, then submit your proof of payment.
5. **Claim your permit** — the office issues the locational clearance (with City Mayor's approval) for you to claim and sign for.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

**For a Zoning Certification:**

- [ ] Duly notarized Application Form (1 original)
- [ ] Proof of Land Ownership or Right (1 photocopy)
- [ ] Latest Tax Declaration (1 photocopy)
- [ ] Lot Plan signed by a licensed Geodetic Engineer (1 original)
- [ ] Vicinity Map signed by a licensed Geodetic Engineer or Architect, clearly showing the exact site and existing land uses/landmarks within at least a 500-meter radius (1 original)

**For a Locational Clearance for Building and Other Structures:**

- [ ] Endorsement letter from the Office of the Building Official (1 original)
- [ ] Duly notarized unified application form for Certificate of Zoning Compliance (1 original)
- [ ] CTC/OCT of the lot (1 certified electronic copy from ROD)
- [ ] Vicinity Map signed by a Geodetic/Civil Engineer or Architect (site and land uses within at least 500 m)
- [ ] Tax Declaration and Tax Clearance/Receipt (1 photocopy each)
- [ ] Notarized Deed of Absolute Sale/Donation/Lease/SPA/Authorization if the proponent is not the registered owner
- [ ] Building plan signed by Civil Engineer, Electrical Engineer, and Master Plumber (4 original sets)
- [ ] Bill of materials and cost estimates, and construction specifications (1 original copy each)
- [ ] CNC/ECC from DENR-EMB if applicable; DAR Conversion Order if within an agricultural zone
- [ ] Affidavit of no objection from neighbors within a 100-meter radius
- [ ] Additional requirements for base stations (NTC authority, DOH radiation report, CAAP Height Clearance, HOA/neighbor consents, barangay endorsement), and for poultry/piggery, cockpits, markets, ricemills, warehouses, cemeteries, funeral establishments, slaughterhouses, etc. (barangay resolution and affidavits of no objection)

**For a Locational Clearance for Business:**

- [ ] Duly accomplished application form for business from the Business Permit and Licensing Office (1 original)

**For a Locational Clearance on Application for Variance/Exception:**

- [ ] Letter of reconsideration applying for exception or variance (1 original) and the decision on the denied application
- [ ] Duly notarized application form for Certificate of Zoning Compliance (1 original)
- [ ] Sets of building plan signed by Civil Engineer, Electrical Engineer, and Master Plumber (1 original)
- [ ] Certified true copy of title (1); notarized deed/lease/SPA/authorization if not the registered owner
- [ ] Sketch plan with vicinity map signed by the Geodetic Engineer (1 photocopy)
- [ ] Tax declaration and tax clearance (1 photocopy each)
- [ ] Bill of materials and cost estimates, and construction specification (1 photocopy each)
- [ ] Structural analysis for two-storey buildings and above (1 original)
- [ ] Affidavits of no objection from adjacent lot owners and from dwellers within 100 m
- [ ] Barangay resolution of no objection; CNC/ECC from DENR-EMB; CAAP and DOH clearances if applicable

### Eligibility

- The Zoning Certification, Locational Clearance for Building, and Variance/Exception are open to all. The Locational Clearance for Business is for business applicants (G2B).

## Fees

**Zoning Certification:** Certification Fee of **PhP 720.00 per hectare or a fraction thereof**, plus a **PhP 30.00 documentary stamp**.

**Locational Clearance (Building):** Fees scale by project type and cost, for example:

- Single residential structure: PhP 300 (P100,000 and below), PhP 600 (over P100k–P200k), PhP 720 (over P200k–P500k), or PhP 1,000 plus 1/10 of 1% in excess of P500,000; plus PhP 200 inspection
- Apartments/townhouses: PhP 1,500 (P500k and below), PhP 2,160 (over P500k–P2M), or PhP 3,600 plus 1/10 of 1% in excess of P2M; plus PhP 200 inspection
- Institutional, commercial/industrial/agro-industrial, and special uses (gasoline station, cell site, slaughterhouse, etc.) have their own brackets, plus PhP 200 inspection
- Alteration/expansion is charged the same as the original application (affected area/cost only)

**Locational Clearance for Business:** The charter lists no fee to be paid (None) in the process table.

**Variance/Exception:** Filing Fee **PhP 5,000**; Locational Clearance Fee of **5% of 10% of the project cost**; Inspection Fee **PhP 1,000**; and a **PhP 5,000 fine** where applicable.

Confirm your exact computation with CPDO–Zoning before paying.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the difference between a Zoning Certification and a Locational Clearance?**
A: A Zoning Certification states your property's zoning classification for whatever purpose you need it. A Locational Clearance permits you to actually introduce development (a building) or establish a business on a parcel of land in line with the Zoning Ordinance — it is a prerequisite for the building permit and business permit.

**Q: Do I need a Locational Clearance before my Building Permit?**
A: Yes. The Locational Clearance for Building is part of the building-permit backroom process, with the Office of the Building Official acting as the client and endorsing the application to CPDO–Zoning.

**Q: When would I apply for a Variance or Exception?**
A: When your project does not conform to the zoning rules and your application has been denied — you may file a letter of reconsideration for a variance/exception, which the Local Zoning Board of Adjustment and Appeals deliberates. This is a highly technical service that takes about 15 days plus a few hours.

**Q: Does my application form need to be notarized?**
A: Yes, for the Zoning Certification and several of the locational clearances. Have it notarized by a Notary Public before submitting.
