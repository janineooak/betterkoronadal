# Access Maternal Care and Child Immunization — City of Koronadal

The City Health Office (CHO) of Koronadal and the barangay health stations provide **prenatal care**, **family planning**, and **free routine child immunization** for residents.

---

## Maternal Care (Prenatal and Related Services)

| Service                                                             | Where                        | Schedule                                 | Requirements                                    | Cost                                                       |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Prenatal check-ups (by midwife/nurse; physician referral as needed) | **Barangay Health Stations** | Weekday clinic hours (varies by station) | Prenatal record (issued/updated at first visit) | **Free**                                                   |
| Family Planning counseling and methods                              | **Barangay Health Stations** | Weekday clinic hours (varies by station) | Personal appearance for counseling              | **Free** counseling (methods per DOH program availability) |

**Tip:** Bring any previous prenatal card or records and a valid ID. If you are new, staff will register you and issue the needed record at the health station.

---

## Child Immunization (EPI)

Routine childhood vaccines are provided **free** under the Expanded Program on Immunization (EPI).

| Age/Visit                          | Vaccines (examples)                                                        | Where                        | When                                           | Requirements                          | Cost     |
| ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | -------- |
| Birth–12 months (per DOH schedule) | **BCG**, **Pentavalent**, **OPV/IPV**, **MMR**, **Hepatitis B**, **PCV13** | **Barangay Health Stations** | On the posted immunization day at your station | **ECCD card** (issued on first visit) | **Free** |

> First time? The health worker will **issue an ECCD card** and guide you on next-dose return dates.

---

## How to Avail (Quick Steps)

1. **Go to your Barangay Health Station** during clinic hours (or the posted EPI day for vaccines).
2. **Register or update your record:** prenatal record for mothers, **ECCD card** for children (issued if first time).
3. **Receive service:** prenatal assessment, counseling (and TT vaccine/supplements per program), or child vaccination per the EPI schedule.
4. **Book your return date** for follow-ups and next vaccine doses as advised by the health staff.

---

## Notes and Extras

- **Medical consultations** at health stations are **free** during weekday clinic hours. This is useful if you need a doctor's assessment alongside prenatal care.
- The CHO and health stations also run **mother's classes** and promote **facility-based delivery** — ask your midwife for schedules.
- For deliveries needing hospital care, residents may be referred to the **South Cotabato Provincial Hospital** in Koronadal or another appropriate facility.
