# Go to the Hospital for Treatment or Confinement — City of Koronadal

This guide covers **where to go**, **what to bring**, and **how admission works** when you need hospital treatment or confinement in the City of Koronadal.

---

## A) Where to Go

Koronadal City is served by the **South Cotabato Provincial Hospital**, located in the city, which provides emergency, in-patient, and out-patient care. Other private and specialty hospitals and clinics also operate in and around the city.

For first-level concerns and minor consultations, the **City Health Office (CHO)** and **barangay health stations** can assess you and provide a referral to a hospital when needed.

In a medical emergency, call **Korona-911**, the city emergency hotline managed by the City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO), or proceed directly to the nearest hospital emergency room.

---

## B) Emergency Care — Quick Steps

| Step | What you do                                  | The hospital does                                             |
| ---- | -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | Go straight to the Emergency Room (ER)       | Triage, vital signs, initial doctor's orders                  |
| 2    | Sign consent (treatment/admission if needed) | Prepare medicines/tests; arrange transfer to ward if admitted |
| 3    | Transfer to the ward (if for confinement)    | ER endorses you to the ward nurse on duty                     |

ERs operate **24/7**. Bring your PhilHealth documents if available, but do not delay seeking care to look for them — staff can guide you on submitting them later.

---

## C) What to Bring (All Hospitals)

- **Valid ID** (with photo and signature)
- **PhilHealth ID and Member Data Record (MDR)**; if you are a dependent, bring proof of dependency
- **Doctor's referral or admitting order** (for planned/elective admission)
- **HMO or company documents:** letter of authorization/guarantee letter and membership IDs (if applicable)
- **Personal necessities** (for confinement)

---

## D) Costs and PhilHealth Coverage

- Hospital fees are posted at the **Billing** desk; **PhilHealth** benefits may apply during admission or discharge processing.
- **No Balance Billing (NBB):** for **indigent/sponsored members**, **senior citizens**, and certain categories in **government hospitals**, no other fees beyond the PhilHealth package should be charged during confinement (subject to policy and facility capacity).
- Private hospitals may require an **initial deposit** (varies by case and room) unless fully covered by HMO or PhilHealth arrangements. Confirm at the **Admissions/Billing** desk.

---

## E) How to Avail — At a Glance

### Emergency (any hospital)

1. **Go straight to the ER** (24/7).
2. **Triage and initial orders** — stabilization and tests.
3. **If admitted:** sign consent, transfer to the ward, and submit your IDs/PhilHealth/HMO documents at Admissions/Billing.

### Elective Admission (planned confinement or procedure)

1. Obtain a **doctor's admitting order** and **referral**.
2. Prepare your **IDs, PhilHealth, and HMO/LOA** (if any).
3. Proceed to **Admissions** for room assignment, forms, and initial billing arrangements.
