# Use Public Facilities Like Gyms, Barangay Halls, or Multipurpose Centers — City of Koronadal

This guide explains how residents and groups in Koronadal City can **reserve and use public facilities** such as gymnasiums, barangay halls, covered courts, and multipurpose centers for events, meetings, sports, and community activities. Depending on the facility, requests are handled by the **barangay** that manages it or by the **City General Services Office (CGSO)** for city-managed venues.

---

## Common Public Facilities

- **Gymnasiums and covered courts** — for sports events, practices, and large gatherings.
- **Barangay halls and session halls** — for meetings, seminars, and community programs.
- **Multipurpose centers and covered pavilions** — for assemblies, training, and special events.

> Availability, schedules, and fees differ per facility and per managing barangay. Reserve early, especially for weekends, holidays, and peak event seasons.

---

## How to Reserve

### Step 1: Identify the Facility and Manager

- Decide which facility fits your activity (gym, hall, court, or multipurpose center).
- Find out who manages it — the **barangay** for most barangay-level facilities, or the **City General Services Office** for city-managed venues.

### Step 2: Check Availability

- Contact the managing office to **check the schedule** for your preferred date and time.
- Confirm any **rules, capacity limits, and applicable fees** for that facility.

### Step 3: File Your Request

- Submit a **letter of request or reservation form** stating your purpose, date, time, and expected number of participants.
- Provide your **contact details** and the name of the responsible person or organization.

### Step 4: Approval, Payment, and Use

- Wait for **approval** of your reservation.
- Pay any applicable **fees** (issued an official receipt) and observe the facility's **rules and clean-up requirements** during and after use.

---

## Requirements

### Documents Typically Needed

- [ ] **Letter of request or reservation form** stating purpose, date, and time
- [ ] **Valid ID** of the requesting person or authorized representative
- [ ] **Proof of organization** (if applying as a group, school, or association), if required
- [ ] Payment for **use or rental fees**, if applicable

### Things to Remember

- Reserve **early** to secure your preferred schedule.
- Follow all **facility rules** on capacity, noise, equipment, and clean-up.
- You may be asked to help **restore the facility** to its original condition after your activity.

> Specific requirements and fees depend on the managing barangay or office and the applicable ordinance. Confirm these when you check availability.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to pay to use a public facility?**
A: Some facilities are free for community use, while others charge a use or rental fee following the city's Revenue Code or the barangay's ordinance. Confirm with the managing office when you reserve.

**Q: How far in advance should I reserve?**
A: Reserve as early as possible, especially for weekends, holidays, and busy event periods, since popular facilities are booked quickly.

**Q: Who manages the gym or covered court in my barangay?**
A: Most barangay-level facilities are managed by the barangay itself. For city-managed venues, coordinate with the City General Services Office.

**Q: Can organizations and schools reserve facilities?**
A: Yes. Residents, organizations, schools, and community groups can all request to use public facilities, subject to availability and approval.
