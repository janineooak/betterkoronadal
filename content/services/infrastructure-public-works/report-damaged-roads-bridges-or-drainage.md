# Report Damaged Roads, Bridges, or Drainage — City of Koronadal

Use this guide to report **damaged or unsafe roads, bridges, and drainage systems** in Koronadal City so they can be inspected and repaired. Reports help the city keep public infrastructure safe and passable. Road, bridge, and drainage concerns are handled by the **City Engineering Office**.

---

## What You Can Report

- **Roads** — potholes, cracks, washed-out sections, missing or damaged road signs, and obstructions.
- **Bridges** — cracks, damaged railings, scouring, or structural concerns that affect safety.
- **Drainage** — clogged, broken, or overflowing canals and drainage lines, and flooding caused by blocked waterways.
- **Other public works** — damaged public structures, road shoulders, or related city facilities.

> For immediate dangers such as a collapsed road, exposed manhole, or a bridge at risk, report right away and warn other residents. For disaster-related emergencies, you may also reach the City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO) through Korona-911.

---

## How to Report

### Step 1: Note the Details

- Identify the **exact location** (barangay, street or purok, and a nearby landmark).
- Describe the **type and extent of the damage** (for example, a large pothole, broken canal cover, or a flooded section).
- If it is safe to do so, take a **photo** to support your report.

### Step 2: Submit Your Report

- Report in person at the **City Engineering Office**, or
- Course your report through your **barangay**, which can endorse it to the city, or
- Send the details through the city's official **Facebook page** or **email**.

### Step 3: Inspection and Assessment

- City Engineering staff will **verify and assess** the reported damage.
- The repair is then **scheduled** based on its severity, safety risk, and available resources.

### Step 4: Repair and Follow-Up

- The city carries out the **repair or maintenance work**.
- You may follow up on the status of your report through the City Engineering Office or your barangay.

---

## What to Include in Your Report

- [ ] **Exact location** (barangay, street/purok, and nearest landmark)
- [ ] **Type of damage** (road, bridge, or drainage) and how severe it is
- [ ] **Photo** of the damage, if it is safe to take one
- [ ] **Your name and contact number**, so staff can reach you for verification (optional but helpful)

> The more specific your details, the faster the city can locate and address the problem.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is there a fee to report damaged infrastructure?**
A: No. Reporting damaged roads, bridges, or drainage is free.

**Q: How long before the damage is repaired?**
A: It depends on the severity of the damage, weather conditions, and the scheduling of works. Hazards that pose an immediate danger are prioritized.

**Q: Can I report on behalf of my neighborhood?**
A: Yes. Residents, business owners, and barangay officials can all report. Reporting through your barangay can help the concern reach the City Engineering Office faster.

**Q: What if the damage is causing flooding right now?**
A: Report it immediately to the City Engineering Office. For disaster-related emergencies, you may also contact the CDRRMO through Korona-911.
