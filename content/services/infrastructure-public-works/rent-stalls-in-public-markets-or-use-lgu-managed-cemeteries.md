# Rent Stalls in Public Markets or Use LGU-Managed Cemeteries — City of Koronadal

This guide covers two public services managed by the City Government of Koronadal: **renting a stall in a public market** and **using LGU-managed cemetery services**. Public market stalls support local vendors and small businesses, while cemetery services help families during times of loss. These services are coordinated through the **City Economic Enterprise Development Office (CEEDO)** and the offices that manage the city's economic enterprises.

## Renting a Public Market Stall

Public market stalls are intended for legitimate vendors and small businesses. Availability depends on vacancies in each market section.

### How to Apply for a Stall

1. **Inquire about vacancies** — Visit the public market office or CEEDO to ask about available stalls and the section that fits your goods.
2. **File your application** — Submit the application form and required documents, including valid IDs and your business details.
3. **Assessment and award** — Once approved, your stall rental and fees are assessed under the city's Revenue Code, and a lease or occupancy agreement is issued.
4. **Pay and comply** — Pay the applicable fees, keep your official receipts, and follow market rules on cleanliness, operating hours, and proper use of the stall.

### Stall Requirements

- [ ] Accomplished **application form** for a market stall
- [ ] **Valid government-issued ID** of the applicant
- [ ] **Business registration or permit** details, as required
- [ ] Payment of **stall rental and applicable fees**

> Stall availability and exact fees depend on the market and section. Vendors must keep their stalls in good standing by paying dues on time and following market regulations.

## Using LGU-Managed Cemetery Services

The city's public cemetery offers burial and related services to residents. Families should coordinate with the managing office to arrange a plot and complete the requirements.

### How to Arrange Cemetery Services

1. **Coordinate with the office** — Visit the public cemetery office or CEEDO to inquire about available plots and the services you need.
2. **Submit requirements** — Provide the death certificate and other documents needed to process the burial.
3. **Pay the fees** — Settle the cemetery and plot fees under the city's Revenue Code, and keep your official receipt.
4. **Schedule the interment** — Coordinate the date and arrangements with the office, and observe cemetery rules and maintenance requirements.

### Cemetery Requirements

- [ ] **Registered Death Certificate** of the deceased
- [ ] **Valid ID** of the requesting family member or representative
- [ ] Payment of **cemetery and plot fees**, as applicable
- [ ] Other documents the managing office may require

> Plot availability, lease terms, and renewal periods depend on the cemetery's rules and the city's ordinances. Confirm these with the managing office.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much does it cost to rent a stall or use cemetery services?**
A: Stall rentals and cemetery fees follow the city's Revenue Code and applicable ordinances. The managing office assesses the exact amount based on your application.

**Q: How do I know if a stall or cemetery plot is available?**
A: Inquire directly with the public market or cemetery office, or with CEEDO, since availability changes over time.

**Q: Can I transfer my stall to someone else?**
A: Stall transfers are subject to the rules of the city's market ordinance. Coordinate with the managing office before making any arrangements.

**Q: What is the first document needed for a burial?**
A: A registered Death Certificate of the deceased is generally required, along with valid identification of the requesting family member.
