# Request Access to Barangay Water Supply Systems — City of Koronadal

This guide explains how residents of Koronadal City can request access or a connection to **barangay or community water supply systems**. Clean and reliable water is essential for every household, and this page helps you understand where to inquire and what is generally required. Water supply concerns are coordinated through your **barangay** and the **City Engineering Office**, with the appropriate **water service provider** for your area.

## Before You Apply

Water service in the city may be provided through different systems depending on your location. To avoid delays, find out first which one serves your area.

- A **barangay or community-managed water system** (often a barangay waterworks or cooperative).
- A **water district or accredited water service provider** covering your area.
- A **Level I or Level II communal source** (such as a shared standpipe or deep well) in areas without piped household connections.

> If you are not sure who provides water in your barangay, ask your **barangay office** first. They can tell you which system serves your purok and how to apply.

---

## How to Request

### Step 1: Inquire With Your Barangay

- Visit your **barangay office** and ask which water system serves your area.
- Request the **application requirements** and the applicable **fees** for a new connection or access.

### Step 2: File Your Application

- Submit your application and supporting documents to the **water service provider** or barangay water system that covers your area.
- Provide your **exact address** and household details so the line can be properly located.

### Step 3: Site Inspection and Assessment

- The provider may conduct a **site inspection** to check the distance to the nearest line and the feasibility of connection.
- Your **connection and service fees** will be assessed based on the provider's schedule of rates.

### Step 4: Installation and Activation

- After payment, the provider **installs the connection** (such as the service line and water meter).
- Once activated, you can begin using the service, subject to the provider's billing and usage rules.

## Requirements

### Documents Typically Needed

- [ ] Accomplished **application form** from the barangay or water service provider
- [ ] **Valid government-issued ID** of the applicant
- [ ] **Proof of residency or property** (such as a barangay certificate, lease, or land document)
- [ ] **Authorization letter**, if the property owner is not the one applying

### Who Can Request

- Households and establishments located within the service area of a barangay or community water system
- Property owners or their authorized representatives

> Exact requirements and fees depend on the water service provider serving your barangay. Confirm these directly with them before applying.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who do I contact for a new water connection?**
A: Start with your barangay office to find out which water system serves your area, then apply with that water service provider. The City Engineering Office can help direct you if you are unsure.

**Q: How much does a water connection cost?**
A: Connection and service fees are set by the water service provider, not by the city. Ask the provider for their current schedule of rates.

**Q: My area has no piped connection yet. What are my options?**
A: Some areas are served by communal sources such as shared standpipes or deep wells. Inquire with your barangay about the available water source for your purok.

**Q: Can I apply if I am renting?**
A: Yes, but you may need the property owner's authorization. Confirm the exact requirements with the water service provider.
