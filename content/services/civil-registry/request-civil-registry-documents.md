# Request Civil Registry Documents — City of Koronadal

Need a copy of your birth, marriage, or death certificate? The City Civil Registry Office (CCRO) can issue local copies and certifications, and can refer your request to the PSA for authenticated (SECPA) copies and CENOMAR. Any citizen may request their own civil registry documents for whatever legal purpose they may serve.

## How to Request

### Step 1: Fill up the Request Form

- Get and complete the Request Form at the CCRO.

### Step 2: Submit the form and your requirements

- Hand in your Request Form, a valid ID, and (if you are not the owner) an authorization letter or Special Power of Attorney. Staff check it for completeness and issue an Order of Payment.

### Step 3: Pay the corresponding fee

- Pay at the City Treasurer's Office (fee depends on the type of copy you need) and secure your Official Receipt.

### Step 4: Claim your document

- **For local copies / certifications:** Present your Official Receipt. Staff verify, photocopy or prepare the document for the Records Division Head's signature, log it, and release it — usually within about 20 minutes.
- **For authenticated (SECPA) copies or CENOMAR:** The CCRO prepares an endorsement to PSA–General Santos. Requests are forwarded every Friday. You will get a claim stub and return after about **7 days** (birth, marriage, death) or **14 days** (CENOMAR) to claim the authenticated document.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Duly Accomplished Request Form — from the CCRO
- [ ] Valid (government-issued) ID
- [ ] Your copy of a previous request, if available
- [ ] Authorization Letter with a photocopy of the authorizing person's ID — if you are **not** a direct family member of the document owner
- [ ] Special Power of Attorney or Court Order — where required

### Eligibility

- Open to all. If you are requesting on behalf of someone else, bring proper authorization.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the difference between a local copy and an authenticated (PSA) copy?**
A: A local certified machine copy or certification is issued by the CCRO directly (about 20 minutes). An authenticated copy (SECPA) and CENOMAR come from the PSA — the CCRO endorses your request and you claim it after about 7 days (birth/marriage/death) or 14 days (CENOMAR).

**Q: Can I request a document for a family member?**
A: Yes. If you are not a direct family member of the document owner, bring an authorization letter with a photocopy of the authorizing person's ID, or a Special Power of Attorney / Court Order.

**Q: How do I get a CENOMAR?**
A: Request it through the CCRO's authenticated-copy (SECPA) process. The fee is PHP 210 and it is released after about 14 days.

**Q: How much is a certified copy of my birth certificate?**
A: A Certified Machine Copy is PHP 50 per copy locally. A CRD certification (Form 1A/2A/3A) is PHP 100 per copy plus documentary stamp.
