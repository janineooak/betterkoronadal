# Correct or Amend a Civil Registry Record — City of Koronadal

Sometimes a birth, marriage, or death certificate has a clerical error, a missing entry, or needs to be updated to reflect a change in a person's status. The City Civil Registry Office (CCRO), through its Court Decree and Legal Instruments (CDLI) section, handles several of these corrections — many without going to court. This page covers correction of clerical errors and change of first name, legitimation, registering a court decree, the Affidavit to Use the Surname of the Father (AUSF), and supplemental reports.

## Which Service Do You Need?

- **Correction of Clerical Error / Change of First Name (RA 9048 / RA 10172)** — fix harmless typographical errors, or correct the day and month of birth or the sex entry, without a court order; or change a first name.
- **Legitimation (RA 9858)** — for a child born outside marriage whose parents later married each other without legal impediment (except age).
- **Registering a Court Decree** — record a court order/decision (e.g., adoption, annulment, correction of entries) at the CCRO where the court operates.
- **Affidavit to Use the Surname of the Father (AUSF, RA 9255)** — for an illegitimate child (born on or after March 19, 2004) to use the father's surname.
- **Supplemental Report** — supply an entry that was inadvertently omitted when a document was registered.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Identify your correction and prepare your documents

- Visit the CCRO's CDLI section so staff can confirm which process applies and give you the right form and checklist.

### Step 2: Submit the form and requirements

- For petitions (RA 9048 / RA 10172), secure the petition form, have it notarized, then submit it with your requirements. For legitimation, AUSF, supplemental reports, and court decrees, present the PSA copy and supporting documents for examination.

### Step 3: Pay the corresponding fees

- Staff issue an Order of Payment. Pay at the City Treasurer's Office and secure your Official Receipt. (Indigent petitioners for clerical-error/change-of-name petitions may be exempt from the filing fee with proper certification.)

### Step 4: Posting, publication, and processing (for petitions)

- A petition for correction or change of name is posted for 10 days (plus review). A change of first name or correction of date of birth/sex also requires **publication** in a newspaper once a week for two consecutive weeks.

### Step 5: Endorsement to PSA and release

- The CCRO registers and processes the document, then forwards or endorses it to the PSA for annotation. For legitimation, AUSF, court decrees, and supplemental reports, the annotated/endorsed document is sent to the appropriate PSA office; you later request the annotated copy from PSA. Petitioners (RA 9048/10172) may need to mail documents to PSA Legal Services in Quezon City and wait for the result (AFFIRMED or IMPUGNED).

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

**Correction of Clerical Error / Change of First Name (RA 9048 / RA 10172):**

- [ ] PSA copy of the certificate with the error (1 original + 4 photocopies)
- [ ] Local copy of the certificate with the error (4 photocopies)
- [ ] Baptismal Certificate (4 photocopies)
- [ ] Supporting records if available: marriage certificate of parents/owner, birth certificates of children/siblings, school records (TOR, diploma)
- [ ] At least 2 valid IDs (4 photocopies)
- [ ] Indigent Certification, if applicable
- [ ] **For RA 10172 (correction of sex or date of birth):** NBI and Police Clearance, earliest school record (Form 137), Certificate of Employment / Affidavit of Non-Employment, Medical Certificate (and, for correction of sex, an ultrasound copy and certification from an accredited government physician); plus Affidavit of Publication and newspaper clippings of the published petition

**Legitimation (RA 9858):**

- [ ] Joint Affidavit of Legitimation (AOL) (1 original + 3 photocopies)
- [ ] PSA Birth Certificate of the child (1 original + 3 photocopies)
- [ ] Marriage Certificate of the parents (1 original + 3 photocopies)
- [ ] CENOMAR / Advice on Marriage of parents (1 original + 3 photocopies)
- [ ] If applicable: Affidavit of Paternity (if the child was not acknowledged), Joint Supplemental Affidavit (if a parent is/was a minor), death/court documents where a parent is deceased or presumed dead

**Registering a Court Decree:**

- [ ] Approved Court Order/Decree/Decision, certified by the Clerk of Court (4 photocopies)
- [ ] Certificate of Finality, certified by the Clerk of Court (4 photocopies)
- [ ] Certificate of Authenticity, certified by the Clerk of Court (4 photocopies)
- [ ] Certificate of Registration from the Local Civil Registrar, certified by the Clerk of Court (4 photocopies)
- [ ] Copy of the civil registry document affected by the court order (4 photocopies)

**Affidavit to Use the Surname of the Father (AUSF, RA 9255):**

- [ ] PSA copy of the Birth Certificate (1 original + 3 photocopies)
- [ ] Affidavit of Paternity if the child was not acknowledged at the back of the COLB (1 original + 3 photocopies)
- [ ] Notarized AUSF form (1 original + 3 photocopies)

**Supplemental Report:**

- [ ] PSA copy of the civil registry document with the missing entry (Birth, Marriage, Death, or Fetal Death)
- [ ] Self-affidavit declaring the missing entry, attested by two disinterested parties
- [ ] At least two documents that support the missing entry and show the owner's correct information

### Eligibility

- Open to all document owners. For court decrees, only the successful petitioner registers, upon advice of the Clerk of Court, at the CCRO where the court operates.
- AUSF applies to illegitimate children born on or after March 19, 2004. (Those born August 3, 1988 to March 18, 2004 who want to use the father's surname must go through judicial proceedings.)

## Fees

**Correction of Clerical Error / Change of First Name:**

- Filing Fee (Clerical/Typographical Error under RA 9048): **PHP 1,000**
- Filing Fee (Change of First Name or correction under RA 10172): **PHP 3,000**
- Service Fee (if migrant petition): **PHP 500**
- Publication Fee: about **PHP 1,500** (local circulation) or **PHP 3,600** (national circulation, for migrant petitions) — at current pricing
- If affirmed: Endorsement **PHP 100 + PHP 30** documentary stamp tax
- Indigent petitioners (as defined under the rules) are exempt from the filing fee

**Legitimation (RA 9858):**

- Legitimation: **PHP 300 + PHP 30** documentary stamp tax
- Endorsement: **PHP 100 + PHP 30** DST
- Form 1A: **PHP 100/set + PHP 30** DST (2 sets)
- Certified Machine Copy: **PHP 50/copy + PHP 30** DST (3 copies)
- Affidavit of Paternity (if child not acknowledged): **PHP 200 + PHP 30** DST

**Registering a Court Decree:**

- Adoption, Cancellation of Civil Registry Document, Civil Interdiction, Correction of Entries, Guardianship, Judicial Determination of Filiation: **PHP 500** each
- Certificate of Registration: **PHP 50**
- Annulment / Declaration of Absolute Nullity of Marriage: **PHP 1,000** each
- Certification Fees (Form 1-A, 2-A, 3-A, Others): **PHP 100** each; Certified Machine Copy: **PHP 50**

**AUSF (RA 9255):**

- Endorsement: **PHP 100 + PHP 30** DST
- Form 1A: **PHP 100/set + PHP 30** DST (2 sets)
- Certified Machine Copy: **PHP 50/copy + PHP 30** DST (3 copies)
- Affidavit of Paternity (if child not acknowledged): **PHP 200 + PHP 30** DST

**Supplemental Report:**

- Endorsement / Miscellaneous Fee: **PHP 200**
- Certification of Documents: **PHP 130**
- Form 1/A: **2 × PHP 100**
- Certified Machine Copy + Documentary Stamp Tax: **PHP 180** (3 × PHP 50)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to go to court to fix a typo on my birth certificate?**
A: Not for harmless clerical or typographical errors, which the City Civil Registrar can correct administratively under RA 9048. Correcting the day and month of birth or the sex entry is allowed under RA 10172 (also without a court order) but needs extra documents and newspaper publication. Other changes may still require a court decree.

**Q: How much is the filing fee for a correction petition?**
A: PHP 1,000 for a clerical/typographical error (RA 9048), and PHP 3,000 for a change of first name or a correction of sex/date of birth (RA 10172). Indigent petitioners with proper certification may be exempt from the filing fee.

**Q: My child was born before we married. Can we legitimate the child?**
A: Yes, through Legitimation (RA 9858) if both parents had no legal impediment to marry each other at the time of conception (except age) and later married. Bring the Joint Affidavit of Legitimation, the child's PSA birth certificate, the parents' marriage certificate, and a CENOMAR/Advice on Marriage.

**Q: Why does my correction take so long to reflect on my PSA copy?**
A: After the CCRO processes and signs the documents, they are endorsed to the PSA for annotation. You then request the annotated copy from PSA — for petitions involving publication and PSA Legal Services, this can take considerable time.

**Q: An entry is blank on my certificate. Which service fixes that?**
A: A Supplemental Report supplies an entry that was inadvertently omitted when the document was registered. Bring your PSA copy, a self-affidavit attested by two disinterested parties, and at least two supporting documents.
