# Register a Birth, Death, or Marriage — City of Koronadal

The City Civil Registry Office (CCRO) records the major events in a person's life — births, marriages, and deaths (including fetal deaths) — in the city's civil register. Registering these documents makes them official and lets you later get PSA copies. This page covers timely registration, registration for children of unmarried parents, and delayed (including out-of-town) registration.

## How to Register

### Step 1: Choose the right type of registration

- **Timely registration** — you are reporting the event within the legal period.
- **Child of unmarried parents** — the birth includes an Affidavit to Use the Surname of the Father (AUSF).
- **Delayed registration** — the event was not registered within the legal period.
- **Out-of-town delayed registration of birth** — the birth happened in another city/municipality but you are filing here.

### Step 2: Secure and fill up the form

- Get and complete the Civil Registry Document (CRD) form. CCRO staff check it for completeness and correctness.

### Step 3: Pay any applicable fee

- Timely registration is free. For the other types, the staff issue an Order of Payment; pay at the City Treasurer's Office and secure your Official Receipt.

### Step 4: Submit and have the document registered

- Present your receipt (if any). The document is entered in the Registry Books and signed by the City Civil Registrar.
- For delayed and out-of-town registration, you may be given a **claim stub** and asked to return after 10 days (posting period). Out-of-town birth registrations are mailed to the city/municipal Civil Registry of the place of birth, and you later receive your copy of the registered document.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

For timely registration:

- [ ] **Birth:** Accomplished Birth Form (4 originals); for unmarried couples/mother, a duly notarized AUSF Form (3 originals) and a valid ID or cedula of the father (photocopy)
- [ ] **Death / Fetal Death:** Accomplished Death Certificate form (4 originals)
- [ ] **Marriage:** Accomplished Marriage Certificate Form (4 originals)

For delayed registration:

- [ ] Accomplished CRD form with the affidavit for delayed registration completed at the back (4 originals)
- [ ] Latest Negative Result of the CRD from a PSA outlet (1 original)
- [ ] Community Tax Certificate / cedula (photocopy)
- [ ] Other evidentiary documents

For out-of-town delayed registration of birth:

- [ ] Filled-up Application Form
- [ ] Authenticated Negative Result of Birth Certificate from PSA (1 original + 1 photocopy, not more than 1 year old)
- [ ] Affidavit for Out-of-Town delayed registration of Birth Certificate (2 originals)
- [ ] Voter's Certification (photocopy)
- [ ] Marriage Certificate (photocopy), Baptismal Certificate if available (photocopy)
- [ ] At least 2 government-issued valid IDs (photocopy)
- [ ] Other evidentiary documents showing birthday and birthplace (e.g., Transcript of Records, service record, insurance policy, barangay clearance, birth certificates of children)

### Eligibility

- Open to all. The event (birth, marriage, death, fetal death) should be registrable in Koronadal City, except for out-of-town delayed birth registration which is forwarded to the place of birth.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is registering a birth, death, or marriage free?**
A: Timely registration is free. Late (delayed) registration has a fee that increases the longer you wait — PHP 100 after 30 days, PHP 200 after 5 months, and PHP 300 after 1 year.

**Q: The birth or death happened in another town. Can I register it here?**
A: For births, yes — through out-of-town delayed registration. The CCRO prepares the documents, and you mail them to the Civil Registry of the place of birth, then receive your copy of the registered document.

**Q: My partner and I are not married. How do we register our child?**
A: Submit the Certificate of Live Birth together with a notarized Affidavit to Use the Surname of the Father (AUSF) and the father's valid ID or cedula. The AUSF registration fee is PHP 200.

**Q: Why is delayed registration taking longer?**
A: Delayed and out-of-town registrations may require a 10-day posting period, so you may be asked to return with your claim stub.
