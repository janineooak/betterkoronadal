# Apply for a Marriage License — City of Koronadal

If you and your partner plan to get married, you must first secure a Marriage License from the City Civil Registry Office (CCRO). Both of you file separate sworn applications, and the license is valid anywhere in the Philippines for 120 days from the date it is issued.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Complete your Pre-Marriage Counseling first

- Attend the Pre-Marriage Orientation and Counseling (PMOC/PMC) at the **City Population Office** and get your Pre-Marriage Counseling Certificate. This is a required document, so do it before you file. (See the FAQ below for details.)

### Step 2: Submit your requirements for assessment

- Go to the CCRO and submit your documents. Staff will prepare and print your Application Form for you to sign, then the City Civil Registrar signs it.
- You will be issued an **Order of Payment** — keep it.

### Step 3: Pay the application fee

- Proceed to the City Treasurer's Office, pay the fee, and secure your Official Receipt.

### Step 4: Return to the CCRO for processing

- Present your Official Receipt. The office issues a **claim stub** and posts your application for the required 10-day (calendar) posting period.

### Step 5: Claim your Marriage License after posting

- Return after the 10-day posting period with your claim stub. You will be issued another Order of Payment for the license fees.

### Step 6: Pay the license fees and receive the license

- Pay the Marriage License fee, Service Fee, and Certified Machine Copy fee at the City Treasurer's Office.
- Present the Marriage License back at the CCRO. It is registered in the Registry Book, signed by the City Civil Registrar, and released to you after you sign the logbook.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

For each contracting party:

- [ ] Birth Certificate (1 original) — local file or PSA file
- [ ] CENOMAR (1 original) with attached receipt — from a PSA outlet
- [ ] Community Tax Certificate / Cedula (1 original or photocopy)
- [ ] Pre-Marriage Counseling Certificate (1 original) — from the City Population Office

Additional requirements for certain applicants:

- [ ] **Widow/Widower:** Death Certificate of the late spouse (1 certified machine copy / PSA)
- [ ] **Foreigner:** Legal Capacity to Contract Marriage (from your embassy); if divorced, a copy of the Judicial/Court Decree, Birth Certificate, and photocopy of passport
- [ ] **Annulled:** Judicial/Court Decree (1 original) and the PSA Marriage Certificate of the previous spouse with annotation
- [ ] **Ages 18–21:** Parental Consent of both parents (1 original)
- [ ] **Ages 22–24:** Parental Advice of both parents (1 original)

### Eligibility

- Both parties must be at least 18 years old.
- File with the CCRO of the city where either or both of you habitually reside.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long is a Marriage License valid?**
A: 120 days from the date of issue, valid anywhere in the Philippines. If you do not use it within that period, it is automatically cancelled.

**Q: What is the Pre-Marriage Counseling and where do I get it?**
A: It is the Pre-Marriage Orientation and Counseling (PMOC/PMC), a required step for all couples applying for a marriage license. It is handled by the **City Population Office** and conducted every Wednesday and Friday. Bring any valid ID with a picture (or a Barangay certification of residency if you have no valid ID). Fees there are PHP 280 for a regular session, PHP 580 for special counseling for a Filipino couple, and PHP 1,080 if one partner is a foreigner. The PMO session runs about 4 hours (8:00 a.m.–12:00 noon) and the PMC about 3 hours (1:00 p.m.–4:00 p.m.). Do this first so you have your certificate when you file.

**Q: Why do I have to wait before claiming the license?**
A: The law requires the CCRO to post your application for a 10-day period before the license can be released.

**Q: I am a widow/widower or previously married. Can I still apply?**
A: Yes, but you must submit additional documents — a Death Certificate of your late spouse, or the court decree and annotated PSA marriage certificate if you were annulled or divorced.
