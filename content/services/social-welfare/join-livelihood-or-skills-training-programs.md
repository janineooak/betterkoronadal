# Join Livelihood or Skills Training Programs — City of Koronadal

The City Government of Koronadal helps residents build new skills and start or grow small livelihoods. Through the City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO) and its partners, you can join livelihood projects and skills training that can lead to jobs, self-employment, or extra income for your family.

## Who Can Apply

- Residents of Koronadal who want to learn a new skill or start a livelihood.
- Priority is often given to low-income families, solo parents, persons with disability, out-of-school youth, and other vulnerable groups.
- Some programs target specific groups or barangays, depending on the partner agency and available funds.

---

## How to Apply

1. **Visit or contact the CSWDO**
   - Ask which livelihood and skills training programs are currently open.
   - Programs may be offered with partners such as the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD), TESDA, DOLE, or other agencies.

2. **Choose a program**
   - Pick a training or livelihood project that fits your interest and goals.
   - Ask about the schedule, location, and what the program covers.

3. **Submit your requirements**
   - Provide proof of identity and residency.
   - Submit any additional documents the specific program requires.

4. **Get listed and attend**
   - Once a slot is confirmed, attend the orientation and the training sessions.
   - Some programs provide starter kits, tools, or seed capital after completion, depending on the program and available funds.

---

## Examples of Programs

Depending on what is currently offered and on partner agencies, you may be able to join:

- **Skills training** such as food processing, sewing, cosmetology, welding, or other trades (often with TESDA accreditation).
- **Livelihood and self-employment assistance**, which may include starter kits or seed capital through DSWD's Sustainable Livelihood Program (SLP) or similar projects.
- **Capacity-building** for community groups, associations, and beneficiaries of programs such as the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps).

> The exact programs, slots, and any starter assistance depend on available funds and program guidelines. Confirm what is currently open with the CSWDO.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I have to pay to join a training?**
A: Accredited government livelihood and skills trainings are usually free. Confirm with the CSWDO when you sign up.

**Q: Will I get tools or capital after training?**
A: Some programs provide starter kits, tools, or seed capital after you complete the training, but this depends on the program and available funds. Ask the CSWDO which programs include this.

**Q: How do I find out when the next training starts?**
A: Contact the CSWDO or follow the City Government of Koronadal Facebook page for announcements of new batches and open slots.
