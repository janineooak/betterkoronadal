# Get Family and Community Welfare Support — City of Koronadal

The City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO) supports families and communities in Koronadal through counseling, protective services, and referrals to programs that help in times of need. Whether you are dealing with a family crisis, caring for a child in difficulty, or seeking help for a vulnerable family member, the CSWDO is your first point of contact.

## Who Can Get Help

- Families and individuals residing in Koronadal who need social welfare support.
- Children, women, seniors, and persons with disability who need protection or care.
- Families in crisis due to illness, loss, abuse, neglect, or other difficult situations.

---

## Services You Can Access

- **Counseling and case management** for families and individuals in difficult situations.
- **Protective services** for children, women, seniors, and PWDs who are at risk or in need of care.
- **Referral services** that connect you to medical, legal, shelter, or other support from partner agencies.
- **Crisis assistance**, including referral to the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) programs such as the Assistance to Individuals in Crisis Situation (AICS) for medical, burial, or other urgent needs.
- **Community-based programs**, such as support linked to the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps) and other community welfare projects.

> Specific programs, eligibility, and any financial aid depend on available funds and program guidelines. Confirm what applies to your situation with the CSWDO.

---

## How to Get Support

1. **Visit or contact the CSWDO**
   - Go to the City Social Welfare and Development Office or call the City Hall trunkline.
   - Explain your situation to the staff or social worker.

2. **Meet with a social worker**
   - A social worker will assess your needs and your family's situation.
   - This helps the office identify the right service or program for you.

3. **Submit the needed documents**
   - Provide proof of identity and residency.
   - Submit any additional documents related to your concern (the office will guide you).

4. **Receive support or referral**
   - The CSWDO provides the appropriate service or refers you to a partner agency.
   - For urgent crisis needs, the office can guide you to programs such as DSWD's AICS.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is family and community welfare support free?**
A: Yes. CSWDO services such as counseling, case management, and referrals are free.

**Q: My family is in a crisis and needs financial help. Where do I start?**
A: Visit the CSWDO and explain your situation to a social worker. The office can assess your needs and guide you to crisis assistance such as DSWD's AICS, subject to eligibility and available funds.

**Q: Can I report a child or family member who is at risk?**
A: Yes. Contact the CSWDO so a social worker can assess the situation and provide the right protective services or referral.
