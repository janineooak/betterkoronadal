# Apply for Senior Citizen, Solo Parent, or PWD Assistance — City of Koronadal

If you are a senior citizen, a solo parent, or a person with disability (PWD), the City Government of Koronadal can help you access the benefits and assistance you are entitled to by law. The City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO) helps you register, get your ID or booklet, and apply for the support programs available to you.

## Who Can Apply

- **Senior citizens** — residents of Koronadal aged 60 years and above.
- **Solo parents** — residents raising a child or children alone, as defined under the Solo Parents' Welfare Act.
- **Persons with disability (PWD)** — residents with a permanent or long-term disability, supported by a medical certificate.

You generally need to be a **bona fide resident of Koronadal City** to apply through the CSWDO.

---

## How to Apply

1. **Visit the CSWDO**
   - Go to the City Social Welfare and Development Office at Koronadal City Hall.
   - Ask the staff for the correct registration or application form for your status (senior citizen, solo parent, or PWD).

2. **Submit your requirements**
   - Bring valid identification and proof that you live in Koronadal.
   - Bring the documents that confirm your status (see Requirements below).

3. **Get your ID or booklet**
   - Senior citizens and PWDs receive an ID and a purchase booklet for discounts.
   - Solo parents receive a Solo Parent ID once their application is verified.

4. **Apply for assistance programs**
   - Ask the CSWDO which assistance programs you may qualify for, such as social pension, discounts, or financial aid.
   - The office may refer you to national programs run by the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD), such as the Assistance to Individuals in Crisis Situation (AICS) or the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps).

## Requirements

### For Senior Citizens

- [ ] Proof of age (birth certificate, valid government ID, or similar document)
- [ ] Proof of residency in Koronadal (barangay certificate or utility bill)
- [ ] Recent ID photos (the office will tell you how many)

### For Solo Parents

- [ ] Proof of identity and residency in Koronadal
- [ ] Documents showing your solo parent status (for example, death certificate of spouse, or other supporting documents accepted by the CSWDO)
- [ ] Barangay certificate confirming you are a solo parent, if required

### For PWDs

- [ ] Medical certificate confirming the disability, issued by a licensed physician
- [ ] Proof of identity and residency in Koronadal
- [ ] Recent ID photos (the office will tell you how many)

> Exact document lists can change based on national guidelines. Confirm the current requirements with the CSWDO before you go.

---

## Available Support

Depending on your status and available funds, you may be able to access:

- **Discount IDs and booklets** for senior citizens and PWDs (for medicines, transport, and other goods covered by law).
- **Social pension** for qualified indigent senior citizens, through national and local programs.
- **Solo parent benefits** under the Solo Parents' Welfare Act.
- **Crisis assistance** through DSWD programs such as AICS, for medical, burial, or other urgent needs.

> Specific amounts, slots, and eligibility depend on available funds and program guidelines. Confirm what you qualify for with the CSWDO.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to pay for my senior citizen, solo parent, or PWD ID?**
A: No. Registration and ID issuance through the CSWDO are free.

**Q: Can someone apply on my behalf?**
A: Yes. A family member or authorized representative can usually file for you, especially for seniors or PWDs with mobility issues. Bring your documents and an authorization letter. Confirm the details with the CSWDO.

**Q: What if I need urgent financial or medical help?**
A: Visit the CSWDO and ask about crisis assistance. The office can guide you to DSWD programs such as AICS, subject to eligibility and available funds.
