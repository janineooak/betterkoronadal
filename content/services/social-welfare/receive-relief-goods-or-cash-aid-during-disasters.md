# Receive Relief Goods or Cash Aid During Disasters — City of Koronadal

When a flood, fire, storm, or other emergency affects your family, the City Government of Koronadal provides relief goods and, when available, cash assistance to help you recover. The City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO) leads relief distribution, working with your barangay and the City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO).

---

## What You Need to Know

| Information         | Details                                                                |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Where**           | Your barangay hall first; the CSWDO coordinates city-wide relief       |
| **When**            | During and after a declared emergency or disaster                      |
| **Requirements**    | Proof of identity and residency; barangay validation of your status    |
| **Cost**            | Free                                                                   |
| **Processing Time** | Depends on the situation; relief is distributed as quickly as possible |

---

## Who Can Receive Help

- Residents of Koronadal whose homes or livelihoods are affected by a disaster or emergency.
- Families validated and listed by their barangay as affected or displaced.
- Evacuees staying in city or barangay evacuation centers.

Priority usually goes to the most affected and vulnerable households, including families with seniors, PWDs, pregnant women, and young children.

---

## How to Get Assistance

1. **Stay safe and follow official advisories**
   - During an emergency, follow instructions from your barangay, the CDRRMO, and Korona-911.
   - If you need to evacuate, go to the designated evacuation center.

2. **Report to your barangay**
   - Inform your barangay officials that your household is affected.
   - Your barangay validates affected families and prepares the list used for relief distribution.

3. **Get validated and listed**
   - Validation teams or barangay staff confirm which families are affected.
   - Bring identification if you have it, so your name can be recorded accurately.

4. **Receive relief goods**
   - Relief packs are distributed through your barangay or at designated distribution points.
   - Follow the schedule and instructions announced by the city and your barangay.

5. **Apply for further assistance, if needed**
   - For larger losses, ask the CSWDO about additional aid, including national programs such as the DSWD's Assistance to Individuals in Crisis Situation (AICS).
   - For fire, burial, medical, or similar emergencies, the CSWDO can guide you on what documents to prepare.

## Requirements

### For Relief Goods

- [ ] You are listed by your barangay as an affected resident
- [ ] Valid ID or proof of identity, if available
- [ ] Proof of residency in Koronadal, if requested

### For Cash or Crisis Assistance

- [ ] Proof of identity and residency
- [ ] Documents that show your loss or need (for example, a barangay certificate of the incident, or a fire/incident report)
- [ ] Other documents required by the specific program

> The exact requirements and the availability of cash aid depend on the situation and on national and local program guidelines. Confirm with the CSWDO.

---

## Important Reminders

- **Relief goods and government aid are free.** Never pay anyone to be included in a relief list.
- **Stay updated** through official city channels, including the City Government of Koronadal Facebook page and barangay announcements.
- **Amounts, slots, and the type of aid available depend on available funds and program guidelines.** Coordinate with the CSWDO and your barangay for accurate, current information.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Where do I go first to receive relief?**
A: Start with your barangay. Barangays validate affected families and coordinate distribution with the CSWDO.

**Q: Is there cash assistance for disaster victims?**
A: Cash aid may be available through city programs or national programs such as the DSWD's AICS. Whether it is available, and how much, depends on the situation and program funds. Ask the CSWDO.

**Q: What should I do during an emergency?**
A: Follow official advisories, evacuate to the designated center if instructed, and contact Korona-911 for emergencies.
