# Join Disaster Drills and Evacuation Planning — City of Koronadal

Take part in the disaster drills and evacuation planning activities organized by the **City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO)** so you, your family, and your barangay know exactly what to do when an emergency strikes. Drills build the habits that save lives during earthquakes, fires, floods, and other hazards.

## Why Drills Matter

- They turn safety knowledge into automatic action, so you respond calmly under stress.
- They reveal problems in advance, such as blocked exits or unclear assembly points.
- They help families and barangays agree on a meeting place and a communication plan.
- They prepare vulnerable members of the community, including children, seniors, persons with disabilities, and pregnant women.

---

## How to Join

### Step 1: Connect with your barangay or the CDRRMO

- Ask your **barangay hall** or **Barangay Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Committee** about scheduled drills in your area.
- Schools and workplaces can coordinate directly with the CDRRMO through the City Hall trunkline to request orientation or to align with the city's drill calendar.

### Step 2: Take part in the nationwide quarterly drill

- The Philippines holds a **simultaneous earthquake drill** each quarter. Watch for announcements from the city and your barangay.
- When the signal sounds, practice **Duck, Cover, and Hold**, then evacuate calmly to the designated assembly area.

### Step 3: Build your household evacuation plan

- Agree on a **primary and an alternate evacuation route** out of your home and neighborhood.
- Pick a **meeting point** where the family will regroup if separated.
- Identify the **nearest higher ground or open space** for floods and the **nearest open area** away from buildings for earthquakes.
- Assign roles, such as who carries the go-bag and who assists children or elderly members.

### Step 4: Prepare your go-bag

- Keep a ready **emergency go-bag** near your exit so you can leave quickly. See the checklist below.

### Step 5: Review and repeat

- After each drill, talk about what went well and what was confusing, then adjust your plan.
- Repeat drills regularly so the steps stay familiar to everyone in the household.

## What to Prepare

### Household Emergency Go-Bag

- [ ] Drinking water (enough for at least 3 days) and ready-to-eat food
- [ ] Flashlight and extra batteries
- [ ] First-aid kit and any regular medicines
- [ ] Whistle to signal for help
- [ ] Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
- [ ] Copies of IDs and important documents in a waterproof bag
- [ ] Cash in small bills
- [ ] Phone power bank and charging cable
- [ ] Extra clothes, blanket, and rain protection
- [ ] Hygiene items, face masks, and items for infants or seniors if needed

### Evacuation Plan Essentials

- [ ] Two ways out of your home and neighborhood
- [ ] An agreed family meeting point
- [ ] Contact numbers written down, including Korona-911
- [ ] A plan for pets and livestock
- [ ] Knowledge of your nearest safe assembly area

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to sign up to join a drill?**
A: For community drills, coordinate through your barangay. Schools and workplaces can arrange schedules with the CDRRMO through the City Hall trunkline. The nationwide quarterly earthquake drill is open for everyone to practice.

**Q: How often should my family practice?**
A: Practice your evacuation plan at least once every quarter, ideally alongside the nationwide drill, and review it whenever your household or routine changes.

**Q: Where is our evacuation center?**
A: Evacuation areas are assigned per barangay and may change depending on the hazard. Ask your barangay council or the CDRRMO for the assembly area and evacuation center serving your purok.

**Q: What should I do during an actual emergency?**
A: Follow your evacuation plan, stay calm, help vulnerable family members, and call **Korona-911** for emergencies. Listen for official advisories from the city and your barangay.
