# Learn About Emergency Response Procedures and Evacuation Routes — City of Koronadal

Knowing what to do in the first minutes of an emergency keeps you and your loved ones safe. This guide explains the basic response steps for common hazards and how to plan your evacuation routes, with support from the **City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO)** and the **Korona-911** emergency hotline.

## Call for Help First

In any emergency, call **Korona-911**. Speak clearly and give:

1. **What happened** (fire, medical emergency, flood, accident, etc.).
2. **Where it is** (barangay, purok, street, and a nearby landmark).
3. **How many people** are affected or in danger.
4. **Your name and a contact number** so responders can reach you.

Stay on the line until the responder tells you it is okay to hang up.

## Basic Response Procedures by Hazard

### Earthquake

- **Duck, Cover, and Hold** under a sturdy table away from windows and heavy objects.
- Stay indoors until the shaking stops, then evacuate calmly to an **open area** away from buildings, walls, and power lines.
- Check yourself and others for injuries; be ready for aftershocks.

### Fire

- Alert everyone and get out immediately; do not stop to collect belongings.
- Stay low to avoid smoke and feel doors before opening them.
- Once outside, move to your meeting point and call **Korona-911**. Never go back inside.

### Flood

- Move to **higher ground** as soon as water starts rising.
- Avoid walking or driving through floodwater, which can be deeper and faster than it looks.
- Switch off electricity at the main if it is safe to do so, and follow evacuation advisories from your barangay.

### Severe Weather (Typhoon or Heavy Rain)

- Monitor official advisories from the city, your barangay, and PAGASA.
- Secure your home, prepare your go-bag, and evacuate early if your area is prone to flooding or landslides.
- Do not wait for floodwater to rise before leaving.

## How to Plan Your Evacuation Routes

### Step 1: Know your hazards

- Ask your barangay or the CDRRMO which hazards affect your purok, such as flooding or ground shaking, so you can plan accordingly.

### Step 2: Map your routes

- Identify at least **two ways out** of your home and neighborhood.
- Choose routes that avoid low-lying areas during floods and narrow alleys that could be blocked during a fire or earthquake.

### Step 3: Identify your assembly area

- Find the **nearest safe open space** for earthquakes and fires.
- Find the **nearest higher ground** for floods.
- Confirm your **barangay-designated assembly area and evacuation center** with your barangay council.

### Step 4: Agree on a meeting point and contacts

- Pick a family meeting point in case you get separated.
- Write down emergency contacts, including **Korona-911**, and keep a copy in each go-bag.

### Step 5: Practice

- Walk your routes with your household so everyone, including children and seniors, knows the way.
- Update your plan if roads, your home, or your routine change.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What number do I call in an emergency?**
A: Call **Korona-911**, the city's emergency hotline managed by the CDRRMO. Give the location, what happened, and how many people are affected.

**Q: Where are the official evacuation routes and centers in my area?**
A: Evacuation routes, assembly areas, and evacuation centers are set per barangay and can depend on the hazard. Confirm the ones serving your purok with your barangay council or the CDRRMO.

**Q: How will I know when to evacuate?**
A: Follow official advisories from the city, your barangay, and PAGASA, and act early. When in doubt during a fast-moving hazard like flooding or fire, evacuate without waiting.
