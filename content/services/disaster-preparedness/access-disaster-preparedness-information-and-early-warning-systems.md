# Access Disaster Preparedness Information and Early Warning Systems — City of Koronadal

Staying informed is the first step to staying safe. This guide explains how to get disaster preparedness information and early warnings from the **City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO)**, your barangay, and national agencies, so you can act before a hazard reaches your home.

## Where to Get Official Information

### City and Barangay Channels

- **City Government of Koronadal Facebook page** for advisories, suspension announcements, and preparedness reminders.
- **Your barangay hall and Barangay Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Committee** for area-specific warnings and evacuation instructions.
- **CDRRMO** through the City Hall trunkline for preparedness guidance and questions.

### National Agencies

- **PAGASA** for weather, typhoon, rainfall, and flood advisories.
- **PHIVOLCS** for earthquake and volcanic activity information.
- **NDRRMC** for nationwide disaster bulletins.

> Always rely on official sources. Avoid spreading unverified posts, which can cause panic and confusion.

---

## How to Stay Connected to Early Warnings

### Step 1: Follow official pages and bookmark sources

- Follow the **City Government of Koronadal** Facebook page.
- Note the websites and advisories of PAGASA, PHIVOLCS, and the NDRRMC.

### Step 2: Save emergency contacts

- Save **Korona-911** on your phone and write it down where the whole family can see it.

### Step 3: Coordinate with your barangay

- Ask your barangay how it shares local warnings, such as through announcements, community alerts, or door-to-door advisories, especially if your area is prone to flooding.

### Step 4: Set up backup ways to receive alerts

- Keep a **battery-powered or hand-crank radio** for updates if power and internet are down.
- Make sure household members know to check on each other and share verified advisories.

### Step 5: Act on warnings early

- When an advisory is issued, prepare your go-bag, secure your home, and be ready to evacuate.
- Do not wait for conditions to worsen before taking action.

---

## Understand Common Warning Signals

- **PAGASA Tropical Cyclone Wind Signals (TCWS)** indicate the strength of incoming winds; higher numbers mean greater danger.
- **PAGASA rainfall advisories (Yellow, Orange, Red)** warn of increasing flood risk; Red means severe flooding is expected or happening.
- **Local barangay alerts** may add specific instructions for your purok, such as when to move to higher ground.

When a warning is raised, follow the recommended actions and the instructions from your barangay and the CDRRMO.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How will I know if classes or work are suspended?**
A: Watch for official announcements from the City Government of Koronadal and your barangay, usually posted on the city's Facebook page and shared through barangay channels.

**Q: Where can I get weather and earthquake updates?**
A: Use official national sources: PAGASA for weather and floods, and PHIVOLCS for earthquakes. The city and your barangay relay relevant local advisories.

**Q: What if the power and internet are down?**
A: Keep a battery-powered or hand-crank radio for updates, and rely on your barangay's local announcements. For emergencies, call **Korona-911**.

**Q: How do I avoid fake news during disasters?**
A: Trust only official government and agency sources. Verify before sharing, and check with your barangay or the CDRRMO if you are unsure.
