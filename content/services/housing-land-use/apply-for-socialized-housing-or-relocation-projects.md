# Apply for Socialized Housing or Relocation Projects — City of Koronadal

This guide explains how qualified residents of Koronadal City can apply for **socialized housing** and **relocation projects**. These programs help low-income families, informal settlers, and households displaced by hazards or public projects find safe, secure, and affordable homes. Applications are coordinated through the **Local Housing Board** and the **City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO)**, in partnership with national agencies such as the **National Housing Authority (NHA)**.

## Who Can Apply

You may qualify for socialized housing or relocation if you meet the general criteria below. Final eligibility depends on the specific program and on validation by the Local Housing Board.

- You are a **bona fide resident of Koronadal City** (residency requirements vary per program).
- You belong to a **low-income or underprivileged household** and do not own other residential property.
- You are an **informal settler family** in a danger zone, public land, or an area covered by a government project.
- You are a household **displaced by a fire, flood, or other disaster**, or by an approved infrastructure or relocation project.
- You are **of legal age** and meet the income ceilings set by the program.

> Eligibility, income ceilings, and priority ranking depend on the rules of each program and the guidelines of partner agencies such as the NHA. Confirm the current requirements with the Local Housing Board through the City Hall trunkline.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Inquire and Get Pre-Screened

- Visit the City Hall and ask to be directed to the **Local Housing Board** or the **CSWDO**.
- Explain your situation (low-income household, informal settler, or displaced family) so staff can identify the program that fits you.
- Ask for the **application form** and the current **list of requirements** for that program.

### Step 2: Submit Your Application and Requirements

- Complete the application form and attach the supporting documents.
- Submit everything to the receiving office for recording and initial review.

### Step 3: Validation and Assessment

- A social worker or housing staff may conduct a **home visit, interview, or social case study** to validate your need and eligibility.
- Your household will be assessed against the program's qualification and priority criteria.

### Step 4: Listing and Awarding

- Qualified applicants are placed on the **beneficiary masterlist** and ranked according to program priorities.
- When a unit or relocation lot becomes available, you will be notified for **awarding, orientation, and signing** of the required agreements.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Accomplished **application form** (from the Local Housing Board or CSWDO)
- [ ] **Valid government-issued ID** of the applicant (and spouse, if applicable)
- [ ] **Proof of residency** in Koronadal City (such as a barangay certificate)
- [ ] **Proof of income** or a Certificate of Indigency, as required by the program
- [ ] **Marriage certificate or birth certificates** to establish household composition (if required)
- [ ] Supporting documents proving displacement (such as a fire or disaster certification), if applicable

### Eligibility Summary

- Bona fide resident of Koronadal City
- Low-income or underprivileged household with no other adequate housing
- Not a previous awardee of a government socialized housing unit (unless allowed by the program)
- Meets the income ceiling and other criteria set by the program

> Bring photocopies of all documents, and keep the originals for verification.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does applying guarantee that I will get a house?**
A: No. Submitting an application places you in the validation and ranking process. Awarding depends on your eligibility, your priority ranking, and the availability of housing units or relocation lots.

**Q: Is there a fee to apply?**
A: No. Filing an application is free. If you are awarded a unit or lot, you may pay a subsidized amortization based on the program's terms.

**Q: I am an informal settler in a danger zone. Can I be relocated?**
A: You may qualify for relocation, especially if you are in a hazard area or an area covered by a government project. Inquire with the Local Housing Board so your household can be assessed and listed.

**Q: Which national agency handles socialized housing?**
A: Many projects are coordinated with the National Housing Authority (NHA) and other shelter agencies. The city's Local Housing Board helps connect qualified residents to these programs.
