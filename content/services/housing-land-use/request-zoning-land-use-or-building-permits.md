# Request Zoning, Land Use, or Building Permits — City of Koronadal

This guide explains how to secure **zoning and locational clearance**, **building permits**, and related approvals before you construct, renovate, or change the use of a property in Koronadal City. Zoning and land use are handled by the **City Planning and Development Office (CPDO)**, while building permits are processed through the **City Architect's Office** and the **City Engineering Office**.

## Which Permit Do You Need

- **Zoning / Locational Clearance** — confirms that your intended use (residential, commercial, industrial, etc.) is allowed in that location under the city's zoning ordinance. Usually required **before** the building permit.
- **Building Permit** — required before any **construction, addition, alteration, renovation, or repair** of a structure.
- **Ancillary and accessory permits** — such as electrical, sanitary/plumbing, mechanical, and structural permits, which are evaluated together with the building permit.
- **Occupancy Permit** — secured **after** construction is completed and inspected, before the structure is occupied or used.

> If you are unsure which clearance applies to your project, ask the CPDO first. Securing your zoning or locational clearance early helps avoid delays in your building permit.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Secure Zoning / Locational Clearance (CPDO)

- Go to the **City Planning and Development Office (CPDO)** and request a zoning or locational clearance.
- Present your **lot documents** and a description of your intended use so staff can check it against the zoning ordinance.

### Step 2: Prepare Your Building Plans

- Have your **building plans and technical documents** prepared and signed by the appropriate **licensed professionals** (architect and engineers).
- Make sure the plans comply with the **National Building Code** and related codes.

### Step 3: File Your Building Permit Application

- Submit your application, plans, and supporting documents to the **City Architect's Office / City Engineering Office** through the receiving counter.
- Your documents will be checked for completeness before being accepted for evaluation.

### Step 4: Technical Evaluation and Assessment

- Technical staff review your plans for compliance, and your fees are assessed based on the **National Building Code** and the city's **Revenue Code**.
- Pay the assessed fees at the **City Treasurer's Office** and keep your official receipt.

### Step 5: Releasing and Inspection

- Once approved and paid, your **building permit** is released.
- Inspections may be conducted during construction. After completion, apply for an **Occupancy Permit** before using the structure.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Accomplished **application forms** for the clearance or permit
- [ ] **Proof of land ownership** (Transfer Certificate of Title) or authorization to use the lot
- [ ] **Tax Declaration** and proof of **paid real property tax** (from the City Assessor's and Treasurer's Offices)
- [ ] **Zoning / Locational Clearance** from the CPDO (for building permits)
- [ ] **Building plans and technical documents** signed and sealed by licensed professionals
- [ ] **Valid IDs** of the applicant and authorized representative (with authorization letter, if applicable)
- [ ] **Barangay clearance** for the project, if required

### Eligibility

- The applicant is the **registered property owner** or a duly authorized representative.
- The intended use is **allowed in that zone** under the city's zoning ordinance.
- The plans comply with the **National Building Code** and other applicable laws.

> Exact requirements depend on the type and scale of your project. Bring your previous permits or related documents to guide the evaluator.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much does a building permit cost?**
A: Fees are based on the National Building Code and the city's Revenue Code, and they vary with the type, floor area, and use of the structure. The processing office will assess the exact amount based on your plans.

**Q: Do I need a zoning clearance before a building permit?**
A: In most cases, yes. The zoning or locational clearance from the CPDO confirms that your intended use is allowed in that location, and it is generally required before the building permit is processed.

**Q: Can I start building while my permit is being processed?**
A: No. You should secure the building permit before starting construction. Building without a permit can lead to penalties and work stoppage.

**Q: What do I need after construction is finished?**
A: Apply for an **Occupancy Permit** before occupying or using the structure. This certifies that the building was completed in accordance with the approved plans.
