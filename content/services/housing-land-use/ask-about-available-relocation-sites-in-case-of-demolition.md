# Ask About Available Relocation Sites in Case of Demolition — City of Koronadal

If your household is facing **demolition, eviction, or displacement**, this guide explains how to ask about available **relocation sites** and the support that may be available to you. Inquiries are coordinated through the **Local Housing Board** and the **City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO)**, often in partnership with the **National Housing Authority (NHA)** and other shelter agencies.

## Your Rights During Demolition and Eviction

Affected families are protected by law during demolition and eviction. While the city cannot guarantee a specific site to every household, the following general protections apply under national housing and urban development laws.

- Eviction and demolition of underprivileged and homeless citizens should follow **due process and proper notice**.
- Affected families are entitled to **consultation** and, where required, to **adequate relocation** arranged by the responsible agencies.
- **Vulnerable households** (such as those with elderly, persons with disabilities, or young children) may receive priority attention.

> The specific relocation site offered to your family depends on the program governing your case and on available resources. Coordinate early with the Local Housing Board to understand your options.

## How to Inquire

### Step 1: Report Your Situation

- Visit the City Hall and ask to be directed to the **Local Housing Board** or **CSWDO**.
- Explain your situation, including any **notice of demolition or eviction** you have received.
- Bring a copy of any notice or related documents so staff can assess your case.

### Step 2: Validation and Assessment

- A social worker or housing staff may conduct an **interview or home visit** to validate your household and your need for relocation.
- Your household details will be recorded and assessed against the available programs.

### Step 3: Discuss Available Options

- Staff will explain the **relocation options** that may apply to your case and the requirements for qualifying.
- If a relocation site is available, you will be guided through the **listing, awarding, and orientation** steps.

### Step 4: Coordination and Follow-Up

- For cases tied to a specific project or partner agency, the city helps coordinate with the **NHA** or other shelter agencies.
- Keep in contact with the office and update your records so you can be reached when a site or schedule is confirmed.

## Requirements

### Documents to Bring

- [ ] **Valid government-issued ID** of the household head (and spouse, if applicable)
- [ ] **Proof of residency** in the affected area (such as a barangay certificate)
- [ ] Copy of any **notice of demolition, eviction, or displacement** received
- [ ] Documents establishing **household composition** (such as birth or marriage certificates), if available

### Who Should Inquire

- Households living in areas covered by an approved demolition or relocation project
- Informal settler families in danger zones or on public land
- Families displaced or at risk of displacement within Koronadal City

> Even if you do not have complete documents, you should still inquire. Staff can guide you on what to prepare and how to proceed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is the city required to give me a relocation site?**
A: Affected underprivileged and homeless families are entitled to due process and, where the law requires, to adequate relocation. The specific site depends on the program governing your case and on available resources, so it is best to coordinate early with the Local Housing Board.

**Q: I received a demolition notice. What should I do first?**
A: Bring the notice to the Local Housing Board or CSWDO as soon as possible so your household can be validated and assessed for relocation support.

**Q: Does inquiring cost anything?**
A: No. Asking about relocation sites and getting assessed is free. If you are awarded a relocation lot, subsidized amortization terms may apply.

**Q: Will my family be prioritized?**
A: Vulnerable households and families directly affected by an approved project are generally given priority, subject to validation and program rules.
