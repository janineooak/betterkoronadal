# How to Get a Business Permit — City of Koronadal

This guide helps entrepreneurs and business owners apply for a new Mayor's (Business) Permit or renew their existing permit through the City of Koronadal's Business Permits and Licensing Office (BPLO).

---

## Who Needs This

Anyone operating or planning to operate a business within Koronadal City must secure a Mayor's/Business Permit. This applies to:

- New businesses (sole proprietorship, partnership, corporation, or cooperative)
- Existing businesses renewing their permit (generally done every January)
- Businesses changing location, ownership, or line of business

---

## Requirements

Typical requirements for a **new application** include:

- Accomplished BPLO application form
- Barangay Business Clearance (from the barangay where the business is located)
- DTI Certificate (sole proprietorship), SEC Registration (partnership/corporation), or CDA Registration (cooperative)
- Contract of Lease (if renting) or Land Title/Tax Declaration (if owned)
- Occupancy Permit / building clearance
- Sketch or location map of the business
- Valid government-issued ID of the owner or authorized representative
- Fire Safety Inspection Certificate (from the Bureau of Fire Protection)
- Sanitary Permit / health clearance (for applicable businesses)

For **renewal**, you will typically also need:

- Previous year's Business Permit and Official Receipt
- Latest Barangay Business Clearance
- Audited Financial Statements or sworn declaration of gross sales/receipts
- Updated Fire Safety and Sanitary clearances

---

## Step-by-Step

1. Secure a Barangay Business Clearance from the barangay where your business is located.
2. Obtain or prepare your registration documents (DTI/SEC/CDA) and supporting papers.
3. Go to the BPLO at City Hall and get/fill out the business permit application form.
4. Submit your completed form and requirements for assessment.
5. Undergo the required inspections — fire safety (BFP), sanitary, and others as needed. Koronadal often runs a one-stop-shop arrangement, especially during the January renewal period, so several offices may be available in one place.
6. Pay the assessed fees and taxes at the City Treasurer's Office (or designated payment window).
7. Claim your Mayor's/Business Permit and post it visibly at your place of business.

---

## Fees and Timeline

Business permit fees depend on your line of business, capitalization, gross sales/receipts, and applicable regulatory fees (fire, sanitary, garbage, etc.), so amounts vary — confirm the exact assessment with the BPLO or City Treasurer's Office. Renewals are generally processed in January each year; applying or renewing early helps you avoid penalties and long queues. Processing time depends on completeness of your documents and inspection schedules.
