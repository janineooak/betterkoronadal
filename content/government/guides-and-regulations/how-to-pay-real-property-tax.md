# How to Pay Real Property Tax — City of Koronadal

This guide helps property owners pay their Real Property Tax (commonly called "amilyar") at the City of Koronadal's City Treasurer's Office, and points you to the City Assessor's Office for assessment concerns.

---

## Who Needs This

Anyone who owns real property — land, buildings, or other improvements — within Koronadal City is required to pay Real Property Tax annually. This includes:

- Residential, commercial, agricultural, and industrial property owners
- Buyers settling taxes as part of a property transfer
- Owners needing an updated tax clearance for loans, transfers, or business permits

---

## Requirements

Typical requirements include:

- Tax Declaration number of the property
- Latest Official Receipt of your previous Real Property Tax payment (if available)
- Property index number or other property identification details
- Valid government-issued ID of the payer
- Authorization letter, if paying on behalf of the owner

---

## Step-by-Step

1. Gather your property details — Tax Declaration number, latest official receipt, or property index number.
2. Proceed to the City Treasurer's Office at City Hall.
3. Present your property details for assessment of the amount due (including any unpaid back taxes, if applicable).
4. Review the computed amount and choose to pay in full for the year or by quarter.
5. Pay at the designated payment window.
6. Keep your Official Receipt as proof of payment — it will be needed for future transactions.

---

## Fees and Timeline

The tax due depends on your property's assessed value and the applicable tax rate, so the amount varies — confirm your exact assessment with the City Treasurer's Office. Real Property Tax may be paid annually or in quarterly installments. Paying early or in advance for the full year may qualify for a discount, while late payment generally incurs penalties and interest. These amounts and conditions vary, so confirm them with the office. For concerns about your property's assessed value, tax declaration, or reclassification, coordinate with the City Assessor's Office.
