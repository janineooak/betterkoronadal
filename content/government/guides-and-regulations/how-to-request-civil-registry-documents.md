# How to Request Civil Registry Documents — City of Koronadal

This guide helps residents request civil registry documents — such as birth, marriage, and death certificates, CENOMAR, and late registration — from the City Civil Registrar of Koronadal.

---

## Who Needs This

You may need this if you are:

- Requesting your own or a family member's birth, marriage, or death certificate
- Applying for a Certificate of No Marriage Record (CENOMAR)
- Filing a late registration of a birth, marriage, or death event
- Securing a local copy of a document, or requesting a PSA-authenticated (SECPA) copy

---

## Requirements

Typical requirements include:

- Valid government-issued ID of the requesting party
- Complete details of the document owner — full name, date and place of the event (birth/marriage/death), and parents' names where applicable
- Authorization letter and a copy of the document owner's ID if you are requesting on behalf of someone else
- For **late registration**, additional supporting documents are usually required (e.g., affidavits, baptismal/school records, medical or burial records, and other proofs of the event)

**Note:** The City Civil Registrar can issue **local copies** of documents registered in Koronadal. For **PSA-authenticated copies** (used for passports, overseas employment, and similar purposes), you may be directed to the PSA or its authorized outlets, as the City Civil Registrar can endorse records to the PSA.

---

## Step-by-Step

1. Determine which document you need (birth, marriage, death, CENOMAR) and whether a local or PSA copy is required.
2. Prepare the required details and IDs (and an authorization letter if requesting for another person).
3. Go to the City Civil Registrar's Office at City Hall and fill out the request form.
4. Submit your request for verification of the record.
5. Pay the corresponding fee at the designated payment window.
6. Wait for processing and claim your document, or follow the instructions given for PSA-authenticated copies.

---

## Fees and Timeline

Fees vary depending on the type of document and the number of copies requested — confirm the current amounts with the City Civil Registrar's Office. Routine requests for existing local records are usually processed quickly, while late registration and document corrections take longer because they require evaluation of supporting documents.
