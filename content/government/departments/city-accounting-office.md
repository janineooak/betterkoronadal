# City Accounting Office — City of Koronadal

The City Accounting Office keeps the official books of the city government, processes payment vouchers and payroll, and ensures that every transaction is properly recorded and supported before public funds are spent.

## Mandate and Functions

Under the Local Government Code of 1991 (RA 7160) and government accounting rules, the City Accounting Office installs and maintains an internal audit and accounting system for the city. Its core functions include:

- Keeping and maintaining the official accounting records and books of accounts of the city government.
- Recording all financial transactions and preparing the financial statements of the city.
- Reviewing, pre-auditing, and processing disbursement vouchers, claims, and payrolls before payment.
- Certifying to the obligation and availability of funds for proposed expenditures.
- Preparing statements of cash advances, liquidations, salaries, allowances, and reimbursements.
- Maintaining records of accounts payable, accounts receivable, and remittances of mandatory deductions.
- Submitting financial reports to the local chief executive, the Sangguniang Panlungsod, and oversight agencies such as the Commission on Audit (COA).

---

## Services Offered

- Processing and review of disbursement vouchers and supporting documents.
- Preparation and processing of employee payroll, salaries, and benefits.
- Certification of availability of funds for obligations and contracts.
- Liquidation and recording of cash advances.
- Remittance and recording of mandatory deductions (such as GSIS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, and BIR taxes).
- Preparation of financial statements and reports for management and audit purposes.

## How to Reach This Office

1. Visit the City Accounting Office at the New City Hall Grounds during regular weekday office hours.
2. Bring complete supporting documents for vouchers, claims, or liquidations to avoid delays.
3. For status updates or document requirements, call the City Hall trunkline.
