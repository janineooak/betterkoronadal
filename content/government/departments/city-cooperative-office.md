## Mandate and Functions

As provided under the Local Government Code (RA 7160) and the Cooperative Code, the City Cooperative Office is responsible for:

- Organizing and assisting residents in forming cooperatives and community-based enterprises.
- Guiding groups through cooperative registration in coordination with the Cooperative Development Authority (CDA).
- Strengthening existing cooperatives through capability-building, training, and technical assistance.
- Monitoring the operations, compliance, and development of cooperatives in the city.
- Promoting cooperativism as a tool for livelihood, savings, and economic empowerment.
- Linking cooperatives to government programs, financing, and market opportunities.
- Maintaining records and data on cooperatives operating in Koronadal.

---

## Services Offered

- Pre-membership education seminars (PMES) and orientation for aspiring cooperative members.
- Assistance in organizing and registering new cooperatives with the CDA.
- Training on cooperative management, bookkeeping, governance, and operations.
- Technical assistance and advisory support for existing cooperatives.
- Monitoring and compliance assistance for registered cooperatives.
- Linkages to government programs, financing, and livelihood opportunities.

---

## How to Avail of Services

1. Visit the City Cooperative Office at the New City Hall Grounds to discuss your cooperative plans or concerns.
2. Attend the required pre-membership education seminar if you are forming a new cooperative.
3. Prepare and submit the documents needed for organizing or registering your cooperative.
4. Coordinate with the office for training, monitoring, or further assistance.
