# City Architect's Office — City of Koronadal

The City Architect's Office designs and oversees the architectural quality of city buildings, public facilities, and civic spaces, helping ensure that Koronadal's structures are safe, functional, and well-planned for residents.

## Mandate and Functions

Under the Local Government Code (RA 7160) and standard LGU practice, the City Architect's Office is responsible for:

- Preparing architectural designs, plans, and specifications for city government buildings and public facilities.
- Advising the City Mayor and the Sanggunian on architectural standards for public structures and civic spaces.
- Reviewing architectural plans of city projects to ensure they meet safety, accessibility, and design standards.
- Coordinating with the City Engineering Office on the design and construction of public infrastructure.
- Supporting the implementation of the National Building Code and applicable design and accessibility laws.
- Promoting good urban design that reflects the city's identity and serves community needs.

---

## Services Offered

- Preparation of architectural plans and design concepts for city-funded buildings and facilities.
- Technical review of architectural designs for public projects.
- Architectural advice and consultation for other city offices and departments.
- Support in developing design standards for public spaces, parks, and civic structures.
- Coordination on accessibility and safety features in city facilities.

## How to Reach This Office

1. Visit the City Architect's Office at the New City Hall Grounds during regular office hours.
2. Describe the design assistance or plan review you need to the office staff.
3. Provide the relevant project details and documents requested by the office.
4. Coordinate on timelines and follow up as advised by the office.
