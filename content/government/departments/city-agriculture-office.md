# City Agriculture Office — City of Koronadal

The City Agriculture Office helps Koronadal's farmers, fisherfolk, and rural households improve their livelihoods through agricultural extension, planting and farming support, training, and access to productivity programs.

## Mandate and Functions

As provided under the Local Government Code (RA 7160), the City Agriculture Office is responsible for:

- Delivering agricultural extension and on-site farm advisory services to farmers and fisherfolk across Koronadal's 27 barangays.
- Promoting and supporting the productivity of major local crops, especially rice and corn, in line with Koronadal's role as an agricultural hub of the South.
- Distributing planting materials and farm inputs such as seeds, seedlings, fertilizer, and fingerlings to qualified farmers and fisherfolk.
- Conducting trainings, demonstrations, and capability-building activities on improved and climate-resilient farming and fishing practices.
- Implementing livelihood, food security, and agri-enterprise programs for rural households.
- Coordinating with the Department of Agriculture and other national agencies on programs, subsidies, and assistance reaching the city.
- Maintaining a registry of farmers and fisherfolk and gathering agricultural data for planning.

---

## Services Offered

- Free or subsidized seeds, seedlings, fertilizer, and other farm inputs for registered farmers.
- Fingerling and aquaculture support for fisherfolk and pond operators.
- Farm and fishery technical advice, pest and disease management guidance, and field visits.
- Farmer and fisherfolk trainings, seminars, and demonstration farms.
- Assistance in enrolling in the Registry System for Basic Sectors in Agriculture (RSBSA).
- Livelihood and agri-enterprise development support for rural households.
- Referrals and coordination for national government agricultural assistance.

---

## How to Avail of Services

1. Visit the City Agriculture Office at the New City Hall Grounds, or coordinate through your barangay office.
2. Register as a farmer or fisherfolk and provide the basic requirements requested by the office.
3. Inquire about the specific program or input you need and complete any required forms.
4. Wait for evaluation, scheduling, or distribution as advised by the office staff.
