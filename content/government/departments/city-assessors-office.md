# City Assessor's Office — City of Koronadal

The City Assessor's Office appraises and assesses all real property within Koronadal for taxation purposes and maintains the official records of land, buildings, and other improvements in the city.

## Mandate and Functions

Under the Local Government Code of 1991 (RA 7160), the City Assessor's Office ensures that all real property within the city is properly listed, appraised, and assessed for real property taxation. Its core functions include:

- Preparing and maintaining an accurate inventory of all real property (land, buildings, machinery, and other improvements) in the city.
- Appraising and assessing the value of real property using the approved Schedule of Fair Market Values.
- Preparing, issuing, and updating tax declarations for real property owners.
- Conducting the general revision of property assessments as required by law.
- Maintaining tax maps and property records to support the assessment roll.
- Cancelling assessments on property that has been destroyed, exempted, or transferred.
- Furnishing the City Treasurer with the assessment roll used to collect real property tax.

---

## Services Offered

- Issuance of new tax declarations for newly declared land, buildings, or improvements.
- Transfer of tax declaration to a new owner after a sale or transfer of property.
- Issuance of certified true copies of tax declarations and property records.
- Issuance of certifications, such as certificate of no improvement or certificate of property holdings.
- Reassessment or revision of property values when warranted.
- Verification of property records and tax mapping information.

> Requirements and any applicable fees depend on the specific transaction. Please confirm the current requirements and amounts with the office before transacting.

## How to Reach This Office

1. Visit the City Assessor's Office at the New City Hall Grounds during regular weekday office hours.
2. Bring the supporting documents for your transaction (for example, the prior tax declaration, title or deed, and valid identification).
3. To confirm what documents to prepare for your specific transaction, call the City Hall trunkline before visiting.
