# City Treasurer's Office — City of Koronadal

The City Treasurer's Office collects the city's taxes, fees, and charges, and safely manages and disburses public funds so that government services and projects can be delivered to the people of Koronadal.

## Mandate and Functions

Under the Local Government Code of 1991 (RA 7160), the City Treasurer's Office is responsible for the proper, efficient, and economical custody and disbursement of the city's funds. Its core functions include:

- Collecting all local taxes, fees, charges, and other revenues due to the city government.
- Taking custody of and exercising proper management over the funds of the city.
- Disbursing city funds in accordance with approved appropriations and applicable laws, rules, and regulations.
- Maintaining and updating the records of all collections, deposits, and disbursements.
- Inspecting private commercial and industrial establishments to ensure correct payment of business taxes and fees.
- Certifying to the availability of funds for proposed expenditures, together with the Budget and Accounting offices.
- Submitting the required financial and statistical reports on the income and expenditures of the city.

---

## Services Offered

- Payment and assessment of business taxes for new and renewing businesses.
- Payment of real property tax (in coordination with the City Assessor's Office for property records).
- Collection of regulatory fees, permits, and other charges of the city government.
- Issuance of official receipts and certifications of tax payment.
- Issuance of community tax certificates (cedula) for individuals and corporations.
- Assistance with tax clearances required for business renewals and other transactions.
- Cashiering and disbursement of approved city government payments.

> Tax rates, fees, and required documents vary per transaction. Please confirm the current amounts and requirements with the office before paying.

---

## How to Reach This Office

1. Visit the City Treasurer's Office at the New City Hall Grounds during regular weekday office hours.
2. Bring the relevant documents for your transaction (for example, your prior official receipt, tax declaration, or business permit details).
3. For inquiries on what to prepare or current amounts due, call the City Hall trunkline before visiting.
