## Mandate and Functions

Under the Civil Registry Law (Act No. 3753) and the Local Government Code (RA 7160), the City Civil Registrar is responsible for:

- Registering all vital events occurring within the city, including births, marriages, and deaths.
- Maintaining and safekeeping the city's civil registry records.
- Issuing certified true copies and transcriptions of civil registry documents.
- Processing late registration of births, marriages, and deaths.
- Receiving and processing applications for correction of clerical errors and change of first name under RA 9048 and RA 10172.
- Endorsing local civil registry records to the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA).
- Issuing the marriage license and accepting applications for marriage.

---

## Services Offered

- Registration of births, marriages, and deaths.
- Issuance of certified copies of birth, marriage, and death certificates.
- Late registration of vital events.
- Correction of clerical or typographical errors and change of first name (RA 9048 / RA 10172).
- Application and issuance of marriage licenses.
- Endorsement of civil registry documents to the PSA, including CENOMAR requests.

---

## How to Avail of Services

1. Visit the City Civil Registrar during office hours.
2. Inform the staff of the document or transaction you need (e.g., registration, certified copy, or correction).
3. Submit the required documents and accomplish the necessary forms.
4. Pay the applicable fees at the designated window and claim your document or follow the given schedule.

> Requirements and fees vary by transaction. Please confirm the exact requirements with the office before proceeding.
