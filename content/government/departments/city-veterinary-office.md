# City Veterinary Office — City of Koronadal

The City Veterinary Office protects public health and animal welfare in Koronadal through animal health services, anti-rabies vaccination and rabies control, livestock support, and meat inspection.

## Mandate and Functions

As provided under the Local Government Code (RA 7160), the City Veterinary Office is responsible for:

- Providing animal health and veterinary services for pets, livestock, and poultry in the city.
- Implementing anti-rabies vaccination and rabies prevention and control programs to protect residents and animals.
- Delivering livestock and poultry services, including vaccination, deworming, and disease prevention.
- Conducting meat inspection and ensuring the safety of meat and animal products sold to the public.
- Monitoring and helping contain animal diseases and zoonotic threats within Koronadal.
- Promoting responsible pet ownership and animal welfare through information drives.
- Coordinating with the Bureau of Animal Industry and other agencies on animal health programs.

---

## Services Offered

- Anti-rabies vaccination for dogs and cats during scheduled drives and at the office.
- Treatment, deworming, and basic veterinary care for pets and livestock.
- Livestock and poultry vaccination and disease-prevention services.
- Meat inspection and certification for slaughtered animals and meat products.
- Issuance of veterinary health and shipping documents for animals, as applicable.
- Advice on responsible pet ownership and animal welfare.
- Response and reporting for suspected animal disease outbreaks and animal bites.

---

## How to Avail of Services

1. Visit the City Veterinary Office at the New City Hall Grounds with your animal or concern.
2. For vaccination drives, watch for announced schedules in your barangay or on the city's official pages.
3. State your concern and provide any basic details about the animal as requested.
4. Receive the service, certificate, or advice provided by the veterinary staff.
