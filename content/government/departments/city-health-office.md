# City Health Office (CHO) — City of Koronadal

The City Health Office delivers basic public health services to Koronadal residents, including free check-ups, immunization, maternal and child care, nutrition, and disease control programs through city and barangay health stations.

## Mandate and Functions

Under the Local Government Code (RA 7160), the City Health Office is responsible for:

- Formulating and implementing the city's public health programs and policies.
- Providing primary health care, medical consultations, and preventive health services to residents.
- Carrying out immunization and vaccination programs for infants, children, and adults.
- Delivering maternal, newborn, and child health care services.
- Managing nutrition programs and monitoring the nutritional status of children and families.
- Conducting disease surveillance, prevention, and control, including communicable and lifestyle-related diseases.
- Supervising and supporting barangay health stations and community health workers across the city's 27 barangays.

---

## Services Offered

- Free general medical check-ups and consultations.
- Routine immunization and vaccination (infants, children, and adults).
- Maternal and child health services, including prenatal and postnatal care.
- Provision of basic medicines for common ailments, subject to availability.
- Family planning and reproductive health counseling.
- Nutrition counseling, growth monitoring, and supplementary feeding referrals.
- Disease control programs such as tuberculosis (TB), dengue, and other communicable disease management.

---

## How to Avail of Services

1. Visit the City Health Office or your nearest barangay health station during office hours.
2. Register at the reception or records desk and present a valid ID (and any existing health records, if applicable).
3. Wait for your turn for consultation, screening, or the specific health service you need.
4. Follow the health worker's advice, including any referrals to a hospital or specialty service when required.
